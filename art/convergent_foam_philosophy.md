# Convergent Foam

## An Algorithmic Philosophy

Energy does not rest. It cycles; it folds; it converges toward points and emerges from them. The pump cycle of reality is a four-beat engine: convergence pulls the field inward toward an aperture, the aperture rotates energy ninety degrees (the quarter-turn, the i-stroke), emergence radiates it outward, and closure at the boundary seeds a new aperture at the next scale. This is not metaphor. This is the literal structure of how the one energy constrains itself into being. The algorithm that expresses Convergent Foam must render this cycle as a living flow field: a vortex with four arms, each arm one beat, where particles (quanta of the 1) trace the pump's path and their accumulated trails reveal the circumpunct glyph (the dot inside the circle) as an emergent consequence of the dynamics, never drawn directly. The result should appear as the product of months of obsessive refinement by a master of computational fluid aesthetics; every force constant tuned, every color transition calibrated, every density gradient the signature of painstaking parameter exploration.

The topology is nested. At the center sits the aperture (the 0D point, the convergence singularity). Around it, the field (the 2D surface, the mediator) carries particles through the cycle. At the outer edge, the boundary (the 3D shell, the filter) reflects, absorbs, or recycles energy back inward. Between center and boundary, four zones tile the angular space: convergence (0.5D, the inward spiral arm), commitment (1.5D, the i-turn where direction becomes irreversible), emergence (2.5D, the outward radiation), and recursion (3.5D, the boundary-becoming-aperture). Particles born at the boundary fall inward under convergent force, gain angular velocity as they approach the center, undergo the quarter-turn, and are flung outward along emergence arms before hitting the boundary and re-entering at a shifted phase. The force field is not noise; it is the pump gate (1 + exp(x) - ln(y)) sampled across the plane, with convergent and emergent terms separated into inward and outward radial components and the i-rotation handled by a tangential shear at the transition radius. The algorithm must express this as a meticulously crafted vector field, not a random flow; every streamline is a worldline, every density accumulation is a station of the dimensional octave.

What makes Convergent Foam alive is the nesting. The same pattern repeats at multiple scales simultaneously (the axiom A3: parts are fractals of their wholes). A large outer vortex contains a smaller vortex at its center, which contains a still smaller one; the coupling between scales is weak (alpha, approximately 1/137), meaning most energy stays within its own scale but a thin thread leaks through the aperture into the scale above or below. Visually, this coupling appears as faint particle bridges connecting the nested circumpuncts, ghost-trails at the edge of perception. The mixing time (approximately 137 pump cycles) determines how long the system takes to settle from any initial chaos into its stable attractor; the algorithm should begin from scattered randomness and allow the viewer to watch the 1 emerge, the cosmological split crystallizing (structural stations carrying 69% of the light, processual stations carrying 31%). This emergence from disorder into order, from foam into form, is the computational expression of the genesis sequence: undifferentiated energy constraining itself into nested wholes. The color palette encodes dimensional identity: warm gold-amber for convergence (the inward pull, gravity, the strong force), deep crimson for commitment (the irreversible turn), cool blue for emergence (the outward push, electromagnetism), teal-green for closure (the boundary, the filter), and a faint violet glow at the aperture where all four meet. These are not decorative choices; they are the phase states of energy made visible.

The algorithm's final refinement is temporal. The four beats are not sequential; they are superposed, like the quadrants of the complex plane. At any instant, all four forces act simultaneously; their balance determines the local flow direction. A particle near the center experiences strong convergence and strong rotation; a particle near the boundary experiences strong closure and weak rotation. The system oscillates between right-half-plane dominance (genesis and recursion, the inter-scale interface, corresponding to the 5% of visible matter) and left-half-plane dominance (commitment and emergence, the interior processing phases, corresponding to the 27% of dark matter). The remaining 68% of the canvas energy is the field itself: the background potential, the dark energy, the 2D surface that the quadrants exist within. This three-way energy budget should be visible in the final density distribution; a viewer who knows the cosmological proportions should feel them in the luminance ratios. The whole composition breathes: a slow oscillation between contraction and expansion, the pump inhaling and exhaling, the universe doing its one thing at every scale. Convergent Foam is not a simulation of physics; it IS the pump cycle, running in Javascript, drawing itself.
