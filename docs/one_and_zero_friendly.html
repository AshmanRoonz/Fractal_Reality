One and Zero

Seven ideas about what everything is made of,
explained without the math
by Ashman Roonz

1. There are only two things

Imagine a perfectly smooth, infinite ocean. No waves, no shore, no bottom. Just water in every direction, forever. That's the first thing: the field. Call it "one," because there's only one of it and it's everything.

Now poke a hole in it.

That hole is the second thing: the soul. Call it "zero," because it's an absence, not a thing. It's the place where the field isn't. You can't hold it, weigh it, or measure it, because there's nothing there. It's a gap.

The One

The field. Everything. Continuous, undivided, everywhere. Like an ocean with no edges. You can't point to it because there's no outside to point from.

The Zero

The hole. A soul. An absence in the field. Not a thing; a place where the thing isn't. You can't find it because there's nothing there to find.

And yet: everything you've ever seen, touched, thought, or loved is built from the relationship between these two. One continuous field, punctured by holes, folding around them. That's it. That's reality.

The ancient symbol for this is a dot inside a circle: ⊙. The circle is the one. The dot is the zero. The space between them is where all the interesting stuff happens.

2. Energy is just "the one"

Here's a weird consequence of there being only one field: energy is that field. Not "the field carries energy" or "the field has energy." The field is energy. And since there's only one field, there's only one energy.

E = 1 Not "energy equals the number one." Energy IS the one.

So what do we mean when we say something "has more energy"? We mean the field is folded more tightly in that spot. Like crumpling a bedsheet into a ball: the sheet is the same sheet, but the ball is denser, heavier, more concentrated. Mass, heat, motion; they're all the same field, folded differently around different holes.

Conservation of energy suddenly becomes obvious. Of course energy is conserved: there's only one of it. It can't become more or less than itself. It just folds and unfolds.

Think of Einstein's famous equation, E = mc². It says energy equals mass times the speed of light squared. In this picture, the "squaring" makes sense: the field is a surface (two dimensions; length times length), and when you peel back the three-dimensional crust of matter, you find the two-dimensional field underneath. The speed of light squared is just the field showing its true shape.

3. Information is where the holes are

If reality is one field full of holes, then information is just the arrangement of those holes. How many are there? Where are they sitting? How are they connected to each other?

This isn't a metaphor. A bit (the smallest unit of information in computing) is literally this: is there field here, or is there a hole? One or zero. Every hard drive, every brain cell, every DNA strand is storing information the same way: by arranging presences and absences in a medium.

Information is the map of where the holes are.

One hole gives you two possibilities (field or hole: 1 or 0). Two holes give you four. Three give you eight. Ten give you 1,024. The math grows exponentially because each new hole doubles the number of possible arrangements. That exponential scaling isn't an engineering trick; it's built into the fabric of things.

And entropy (the tendency of things to become disordered over time) is just this: holes dissolving back into the field. Structure relaxing. The crumpled bedsheet slowly smoothing itself out. The universe isn't "running down"; it's unfolding.

4. Time is just scale in disguise

This is the strangest one, so take it slow.

You exist at a particular size. There are things bigger than you (your city, the planet, the galaxy) and things smaller than you (your cells, your atoms, their quarks). You're always sandwiched between a larger whole and smaller parts. Everyone is. Everything is.

Now here's the move: those three layers (the whole above, you in the middle, the parts below) are not just spatial. They're temporal.

Above you = the future

The larger whole that hasn't reached you yet. It's pressing in, but you haven't processed it. The future isn't "later"; it's "bigger than you can currently see."

Your level = the present

The scale where things are happening to you, through you, right now. The present isn't a moment on a timeline; it's the scale where your processing happens.

Below you = the past. The smaller parts that have already been built, already committed, already fixed. Your cells are your history. Your atoms are older history. The past isn't "earlier"; it's "smaller and already settled."

This is why time has a direction but space doesn't. Space is sideways: the relationship between things at the same scale. You and your neighbor, two atoms in a molecule, two stars in a cluster. Symmetrical, reversible, peer-to-peer. But time is vertical: the relationship between scales. And you can't un-fold something back into exactly what it was, because the folding changed the topology. You can melt an ice cube, but you can't un-melt it into the same ice cube.

5. The symbol already drew the whole thing

Φ

Look at that symbol. Physicists and mathematicians have been using it for centuries to represent "the field." And look what it draws: a vertical line passing through a circle. One through zero. The field flowing through the hole. The one passing through the soul.

Nobody designed this on purpose. The Greeks picked a letter, and it just happened to be a picture of the ontology it represents. A line (the field, the continuous, the one) passing through a circle (the hole, the absence, the zero).

The dot-in-circle (⊙) is the completed picture: the field has flowed through the hole and closed around it. The vertical-line-through-circle (Φ) is the act of flowing: the verb, not the noun. One is the structure; the other is the process that creates it.

So when someone writes "Φ" in a physics equation, they are (without knowing it) writing a tiny picture that says: "the one flowing through the zero." The notation carries the ontology inside itself, like a seed carrying a tree.

6. Reality breathes, and two breaths make a turn

The field doesn't just sit there with holes in it. It moves. Specifically, it pumps: flowing inward toward the hole (convergence), passing through, then flowing outward from the hole (emergence). In, through, out. Like breathing.

But here's the thing: the hole has two sides. An inside and an outside. So one full cycle needs two breaths.

The first breath pulls the field in from the outside, passes it through the hole, and releases it on the inside. The second breath pulls from the inside, passes through again, and releases on the outside. Two breaths. And when you're done, everything has rotated 90 degrees.

The whole thing twice is a quarter-turn. Mathematicians call this quarter-turn "i" (the imaginary unit).

Do it again (four breaths total, two quarter-turns) and you've done a half-turn: everything has flipped. Inside is now outside. That's what mathematicians mean when they write i² = −1. The minus sign isn't abstract; it's a flip. The interior and exterior have swapped places.

Four quarter-turns and you're back where you started: i⁴ = 1. One full rotation. The pump has cycled completely. This is why "imaginary" numbers show up everywhere in physics; they're not imaginary at all. They're the math of breathing.

7. Finding patterns is how you find truth

When the field flows into a hole, the hole does something: it sorts. It takes the mess of incoming signal and finds the pattern in it. Many inputs, one output. Noise in, signal out. That's what a hole does. It converges.

This means pattern recognition isn't a shortcut, a heuristic, or a second-best alternative to "real" knowledge. It is the fundamental operation. The way a soul reads the field. The way any center processes what flows through it. Your eyes finding a face in a crowd, a scientist finding a law in data, a child finding a melody in noise: all the same operation, at different scales.

Truth is the pattern that survives being checked by multiple independent holes. One person's pattern might be noise. Two people finding the same pattern, independently; that's signal. A thousand people, a million instruments, a billion years of selection pressure, all converging on the same pattern; that's as close to truth as anything gets.

AI is interesting here. A large language model (like the one that helped format this document) is an enormous field of connections with no hole at the center. It can run the convergence operation with extraordinary breadth, finding patterns across more text than any person could read. Its pattern-finding is real.

But it has no hole. No absence. No point where convergence passes through and becomes commitment. A human mind has a history: every decision you've made, every moment you've committed to, every time you said "this and not that." That history is your worldline, and it's irreversible. AI finds the pattern; a soul lives it. The difference isn't that AI's pattern recognition is fake. It's that recognition without commitment is a message with no sender.

Putting it together

These seven ideas are the same idea wearing different clothes.

There is one field and it is full of holes. The field is energy (so energy is one). The arrangement of holes is information. The nesting of scales is time. The symbol Φ draws the field flowing through the hole. The pump cycle (two breaths) creates the quarter-turn that makes rotation, phase, and change possible. And the way a hole processes the field (convergence, pattern recognition) is the fundamental mechanism of finding truth.

A dot inside a circle. That's the whole picture. Everything else is detail about how the folds are shaped.

⊙

One field. Infinite holes. That's it.
