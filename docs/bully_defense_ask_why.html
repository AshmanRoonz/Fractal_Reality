The Deeper Pattern

You don't have to prove you're not lame, your poem isn't cringe, your shoes aren't embarrassing. They have to prove their claim has any content at all. Usually it doesn't.

This connects to a fundamental geometric truth: lies are unstable structures. They collapse under sustained attention because they have no foundation. Truth doesn't need to be defendedâ€”just witnessed. When you ask "why," you're not fighting the lie. You're simply holding space for it to reveal its own emptiness.

The lie collapses. You remain.
