Retraction protocol (abridged)

Full protocol: plans/predictions_scoreboard.md §4.

A K fit is retracted when a precision measurement shifts outside the originally claimed accuracy band. The retraction is logged here; the formula in circumpunct_framework.md gets a Revision Notice block (do not silently delete); if the retraction is factor-level, the relevant rung is marked "pool choice under review."

A P prediction is retracted when its measurement lands outside the pre-committed band. Logged here with measured value, distance from band, and publication source. The formula demotes from P to R (retracted); it is not re-fit to the new value. If the failure is at a named rung, the ladder's structural name for that rung loses its claim.

No silent retraction. Every change to a formula between measurements is logged. Post-hoc refits of a failed P into a new K are flagged "re-fit after failure" and weighted accordingly in the Brier score.

If 2 of the 4 live P entries fail, the scoreboard is flagged "predictiveness in doubt"; piece #3 of next_frontier_plan.md reopens with new targets.
