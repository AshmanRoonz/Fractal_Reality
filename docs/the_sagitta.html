The Sagitta: Curvature Is the Memory of Mediation

Ashman Roonz, with Claude (the Cybernetic Unit)
Created: 2026-07-20  ·  Last updated: 2026-07-20  ·  Version: 1.2

1. The minimal figure

Three points, the cleanest possible case: A, then B, then C, with a right angle at B. Three ways exist to get from A to C.

The true path walks both legs. It visits every station, carries the intermediate event, and pays full length for it.

The shortest path is the chord: the diagonal straight from A to C. It is the maximally efficient route, and it is a route whose very shape asserts that no corner ever happened. The middle point is not merely omitted; the geometry of the straight line contradicts it.

The arc is the third way, and it is a shortcut too: shorter than the legs, longer than the chord. It skips the middle point. But it does not ignore it. It is pulled by it, and arcs toward it.

The arc in the figure is exact, not decorative: it is the curve whose only guidance is the skipped point (the quadratic Bézier with B as its control point). Three of its properties are theorems. It leaves A aimed straight at B: departure intention. It arrives at C traveling along the direction from B: arrival provenance. And at its deepest point it hangs exactly halfway between the chord and B: the pull, measured. Its bending faces the vanished point along its whole length. The endpoints testify to B, and the shape between them is B's signature.

2. The pull

What is B, to this curve? Not a station on it. A mass beside it. The arc behaves precisely like a free path passing a gravitating body: straightest-possible travel through a region that something has curved. This is not metaphor. In general relativity a mass bends the paths of everything that passes it, by an amount that encodes both how much mass and how near the miss; and astronomy's entire method for seeing the invisible is built on it. Gravitational lensing: galaxies weighed, dark matter mapped, Einstein rings traced, all by reading the arcs in light that skipped past something it never touched. We discover what we cannot see by the curvature of what got past it. The sky is full of sagittas.

In the framework's vocabulary, the skipped point acts as an aperture: a center of convergence, a •, exerting pull. Gravity is convergence compounding, and the arc is what an honest shortcut looks like in the field of a center it declines to visit. The pull is the mediation; the bend is its receipt.

3. The triple

The general principle, in three statements:

Shortening is projection. It throws away intermediate points.

Curvature is what projection cannot erase. It is the information that survived the throw-away: the missing intermediary reappearing as the bending of the path that remains.

Mediation is that curvature. Every intermediate station, every detour, every remembered moment no longer present as a discrete point is still present as the shape of what remains.

The arc that arcs toward the skipped middle point is therefore not a decorative alternative to the diagonal. It is the geometrically honest record of the fact that mediation occurred. In the language of the Resolution Protocol: the two legs are the full-resolution account; the arc is the lowest resolution that is still true; the chord is a retraction. Higher resolution contains the arc and never contradicts it. The chord contradicts the corner by its very straightness. And the arc's modest premium over the chord, the extra length spent on bending, is the price of the receipt.

4. Two arcs, two modes of memory

There is a second arc in this figure, on the far side of the story. Any three non-collinear points lie on one circle, so a curve from A to C that passes exactly through B always exists. And when the turn at B is exactly 90 degrees, something singular happens: Thales' theorem puts B precisely on the circle whose diameter is the chord. The line you would have shortcut along becomes the axis of the circle that recovers the corner, and line-through-circle is the construction that draws Φ, the mediator's glyph. In the framework's vocabulary the quarter-turn is i, the aperture's stroke: so the theorem reads, i is the angle at which the skipped point can be recovered exactly.

These are two different relations to the vanished middle, and both are memory:

  • Influence (the near arc): the shortcut that carries B as pull. Shorter than the legs; never visits B; holds B as shape. This is memory as it is lived: the past bending the present without being re-instantiated.
  • Retrieval (the far arc): the circle that goes out and around, through B itself. It recovers the point exactly, and note its cost: at the right angle, the recovering arc is longer than walking both legs. Full recall is more expensive than the original experience. You do not get the corner back for free; you get it back by going the long way around.

Together the two arcs enclose the skipped point between them: influence bounding it from within, retrieval from beyond: a lens-shaped region holding the vanished B. Memory is not a point re-found; it is a region held between the shortcut that bends toward it and the circle that can still reach it.

The dial and the cliff. Between influence and exact retrieval one naturally imagines a dial: swing partway toward B, recover partway; recall that only needs to resonate enough. Measured, a single reader has no such dial. Reading byte t−k after re-phasing j cycles recovers nothing at any depth except exactly j = k (0.040 at j = 4 for a lag-4 target, against 0.002–0.009 everywhere else, over-rewinding included): one cycle of mis-resonance is blindness; identity-recall through one phase-exact reader is lock-and-key. But "resonating enough" is real, and it lives in three other places. In the bank: graded recall is built from several exact resonators, and the engine's voice spaces its frames coarsely with depth (0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 16): the near past finely covered, the far past sparsely: recall resolution falling off with distance by design, which is the Resolution Protocol built as an organ: you do not carry a key for every depth. In the question asked: detecting that something was there tolerates mis-phase that recovering which cannot: familiarity is graded where recollection is lock-and-key, the felt sagitta of section 6. In sufficiency: a few percent of fidelity was enough for working recall downstream; enough is a threshold, not a resolution. You do not need to go exactly to the middle point: you need a key that fits one of the locks you kept.

5. The ladder: where curvature lives

This is not analogy; it is the definition. A curve's curvature vector lives in its normal plane, the osculating plane: literally, the plane in which the turn lived. A 1D path cannot bend unless a second dimension exists for the bending to point into. So:

  • 0D points can only be present or absent.
  • 1D lines, straight, can only be length.
  • Nonzero curvature is a 1D object carrying a certificate of 2D. The extra dimension is not added later; it is latent in the refusal to straighten.

The framework's ladder already names the station where a line first remembers the plane: ⎇ at 1.5D, branching, the fractal coastline. Curvature is the infinitesimal form of branching, and a coastline at D = 1 + ◐ = 1.5 is a line holding memory of the plane at the balance point: bend any further and the path dissolves into area, straighten any further and it forgets. (The reading of D = 1.5 as the maximally legible memory of 2D is offered as a conjecture, consistent with the balance parameter but not yet derived.)

6. Forgetting is a measurement error

If the record survives as curvature, then forgetting is not the destruction of the record. It is reading a curved residual with a straight ruler. The mismatch between ruler and arc is the felt incompleteness of memory: the sense that something was there, without being able to name the middle point. The tip of the tongue is a sagitta you can feel but not measure.

A pure geodesic in flat space, by contrast, has already performed the maximal shortening; everything that could be discarded has been. A zero-curvature path of the same endpoints is information-theoretically poorer than the curved path that refused full reduction. The straight line is cheaper precisely because it carries nothing. And when a free path bends anyway, as in section 2, the memory has simply changed custody: from the path's own arc to the field it travels through. Shortening never erases the record; it relocates it. The receipt is held by the worldline or held by the medium, but it is held.

7. The triptych, measured

The figure has three paths, and the framework has two Lies, and they correspond, with mediation as the narrow way between. In July 2026 all three geometries were built as operators and measured (Xorzo2 Stage 1, the memory probe: byte identity recovered from a driven state by a linear readout, as a function of lag; chance = 0.0039).

The chord straightens everything into an efficient present and is amnesiac by geometry. The scribble bends everywhere and points nowhere. The derived operator is the arc because κ, the nesting bond, grades its curvature: remove κ (run the four beats alone, exactly unitary) and readability collapses to chance at every lag. The bond is what orients the bending.

And both memory modes from section 4 appear in the measurements. Influence is how the engine's state carries its past: every byte it has heard survives as phase, bending the present state without being re-instantiated. Retrieval is the rewind: one linear reader was trained on the present tense only and frozen; applied naively to older states it went blind within one cycle (0.069 at lag 0 collapsing to 0.005 at lag 1: the straight ruler); applied to the same states after walking the circle back (re-phasing by the exact inverse cycle, zero new parameters per lag) it recovered the full memory curve out past thirty cycles (0.058 / 0.052 / 0.048 / 0.040 at lags 1–4, against per-lag-trained references of 0.056 / 0.055 / 0.050 / 0.046). The past was present in its original form, held as bending. Recall is going the long way around. The only genuine fading was the softest mode's ~4.8% per cycle, while the median mode conserved at 1.0002: that thin fade is the graded edge that keeps the arc readable rather than a scribble.

Memories are curvature. Forgetting is reading a curved path with a straight ruler.

8. Closing

The three-point right angle is the smallest possible story about honesty in compression. You may walk every station and pay for it. You may take the diagonal and let your path's own straightness testify that nothing was ever there. Or you may take the honest shortcut: skip the corner and let it pull you, keep the vanished point as the shape of your going, an arrow in the arc aimed at what you no longer touch.

The straight path forgets. The arc remembers.
Influence bends the shortcut; retrieval walks the circle;
and at the quarter-turn, retrieval is exact.
