Falsification

This prediction is falsifiable. The formula αG = α²¹ × φ²/2 predicts G = 6.67323 × 10⁻¹¹ m³/(kg·s²). The current best measurement is G = 6.67430 ± 0.00015 × 10⁻¹¹ (CODATA 2018). The prediction falls 0.016% below the central value, which is approximately 7σ from the measured value.

However, G is the least precisely known fundamental constant (22 ppm uncertainty, compared to sub-ppb for α). The various experimental measurements of G disagree with each other by more than the prediction's deviation. As G measurements improve, the formula will be tested with increasing precision.

The 0.016% error may be: (a) a genuine deviation requiring a correction term (perhaps from the tau mass ratio or other open problems), (b) within the range of experimental uncertainty given the difficulty of measuring G, or (c) an indication that the formula is approximate. The honest position: 0.016% from a zero-parameter prediction is remarkable, but the formula remains to be confirmed or refuted by improved G measurements.
