The Third Option

On lying, truth-telling, and the path most people don't see

A note on resolution

The trap

Most people think they have two choices when truth gets uncomfortable.

DUMP or LIE

That binary is where lying gets its grip. If those are the only two paths, and one feels brutal, the other starts looking necessary.

It isn't.

There is a third option.

The third option

Adjust the resolution of your truth-telling.

Tell more or less, depending on context. Without saying anything false.

  • Say less without saying anything false
  • Leave things out without claiming they aren't there
  • "I'd rather not get into it," and mean it
  • Answer the simple version; let them ask for more

Once you see the third option clearly,

the lie becomes unnecessary.

You had a graded path the whole time. The binary was just loud.

But not every liar is the same

Three kinds of liars look identical on the outside.

Three different stories underneath. The same behavior; the same words; very different moral weight.

First kind

The Willful Liar

Saw the third option. Ducked it.

Had the better path right there and went the worse way. Foolish (chose worse over better) and weak (couldn't hold the harder thing).

This is the kind of liar contempt fits.

Second kind

The Wounded Liar

Grew up where lying was the only way to stay safe.

Abuse. Manipulation. Families where being honest got punished. Lying got installed before they were old enough to choose anything.

Calling them weak fools is hitting someone for limping when you don't know they were broken.

Third kind

The Unshown Liar

Never knew the third option existed.

Was taught the binary and believed it. When someone shows them the graded path, they get it.

Not weak. Uninformed. Needs a teacher, not contempt.

Three stories, one behavior

Check which kind before you decide what they have earned.

The discipline

The discipline is not "never lie and despise everyone who does."

I do not need lies, because I can adjust the resolution of my truth instead.

And when I look at someone who lies, I check which kind of liar they are before I decide what they have earned.

Truth-telling doesn't make you righteous.

It makes you free.

And freedom that holds contempt for the unfree is just a louder kind of unfreedom.
