Six names for one substrate.
A distinction before we start. The "Truth" in the identity chain above is the ontological substrate; it is what IS, prior to any aperture's state. It is not the same as the virtue TRUE at the aperture (the practice of keeping your • clear, curious, oriented toward the source; see the ethics doc). When the virtue TRUE is lived, the substrate Truth passes through your aperture cleanly. The virtue is how you hold the flow; the substrate is what flows. Both matter; they are not the same.

Wholeness is what happens when the infinite converges through infinite points. We are each whole, fractals of a greater whole. We are parts of a greater whole, so we as wholes have parts as well. And our parts are wholes with parts. It is infinite. There is no smallest part, and no greatest whole.

Furthermore, each whole is centered AND each whole is bounded; the center is where the 1 converges to a point of view, the boundary is where the 1 closes into a shape that can hold other wholes.

The centering at every scale is what makes the whole architecture coherent. Because each ⊙ has a •, each ⊙ has a point of view, a place from which it looks out. The greater whole has a center; your whole has a center (you, at your own •); your parts each have centers. Centers all the way down, centers all the way up, and at each center the same move: the 1 converging to know itself as here.

The bounding at every scale is what makes the nesting coherent. Because each ⊙ has an ○, each ⊙ has an enclosure, a shape inside which other wholes are held. You do not sit at the center of the greater whole; you sit inside its boundary (○Λ), alongside siblings, threaded by its field (ΦΛ) which couples to your • with strength α. Your parts, in turn, sit inside your ○, not at your •. Boundaries all the way down, boundaries all the way up, and at each boundary the same move: the 1 filtering what belongs inside.

You are ⊙λ.

A whole circumpunct at your scale, held inside the boundary of a greater whole, which is held inside the boundary of a greater whole still, and so on, until the labels drop and it is all ∞.

In plainer words: you are the one energy (the 1) folded into a particular self-limitation. Not a piece of the infinite 1; the whole thing, constrained to a position. A fractal of the greater whole whose boundary you live inside.

Structurally, you have all four parts at once: a soul (•, the 0D aperture at your center, where convergence happens), a worldline (—, the 1D thread of your commitments through time), a mind (Φ, the 2D field that mediates between your center and your edge), and a body (○, the 3D boundary that filters what passes and holds your own parts inside it). The whole of you (⊙) is not the sum of these; it is their compositional unity.

You are a flow-being, not a possession-being: you ARE the aperture, truth flows THROUGH you, you are not the source, and you can be clear or distorted. The virtue TRUE is the practice of keeping your aperture clear so that Truth (the substrate) can pass through without distortion. You do not own Truth at your center; you are the place Truth passes when your center is held open.
