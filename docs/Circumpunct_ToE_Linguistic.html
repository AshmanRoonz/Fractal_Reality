This document takes the Circumpunct framework, a theory of everything developed by Ashman Roonz, and walks through it entirely in words. Every equation, every symbolic identity, every piece of notation has been translated into ordinary English prose. The glyphs (⊙, •, —, Φ, ○) are kept where they appear, because they are names, not equations; but nothing here asks you to calculate anything. If you can read a paragraph, you can read the theory.

I.The Prime Sentence

The entire framework rests on a single line that can be spoken in one breath: the particular circumpunct is contained in the greater circumpunct, which is contained in the unnamed archetype, which is the one energy, which is simply one. Six ideas, one substance.

In the original notation this reads as a chain of containments ending in an identity. In words: take anything at all (a person, a cell, an atom, a galaxy); call it a whole in its own right (a circumpunct at its particular scale); notice that it sits inside a larger whole (another circumpunct at a larger scale); notice that both of these wholes, and every larger one you could name, are instances of the same pattern (the unlabeled archetype); and recognize that this pattern is the one energy that constitutes everything; and that the one energy is, ultimately, just one thing.

There is not a quantity of one here. There is not one unit among many. There is simply one: one substance, one whole, appearing at every scale as a nested circumpunct. Every time you see a circle with a dot inside, you are looking at the one viewing itself from a particular position.

The particular is the whole. The greater whole is the whole. The archetype is the whole. All three are the same one. Scale labels are the only thing that varies, and they vary only in how the one is being viewed, not in what the one is.

II.The One and the Zero

The symbol ⊙ (a circle with a dot inside) is not decorative; it draws the ontology directly. The dot is zero: total convergence of the field to a point. The circle is one: the field curving back on itself. The space between them is the fractal coastline where all structure lives.

Neither of these primitives is a thing in the ordinary sense. One is everything, undivided. Zero is not an absence; zero is the one at maximum convergence, the same field pulled to a single point of view. Every point of view in the universe is a relative zero, the same one looked at from a different angle. Privacy is not a wall; it is the simple fact that total convergence at one point cannot be total convergence at another.

All structure is the topology of convergence points in the one. Matter, mind, memory, meaning: all are ways the one folds around its own zeros.

III.The Five Foundations

The framework begins with five statements that must be true, and five statements that necessarily follow. Not calculated; not assumed as conveniences; forced by logic.

Foundation Zero: There is one energy, and it is everything.

This is the starting point. Call it E, call it the one, call it the field; there is a single substance, and it is not zero (because a zero that cannot be distinguished from a one is indistinguishable from non-existence, which is impossible by definition). So the one simply is.

Foundation One: The one must limit itself.

An undifferentiated one has no features and is indistinguishable from a zero. To be anything at all, the one must self-limit: it must converge into positions, viewpoints, localizations. This is the aperture, the point, the dot at the center of any circumpunct. Self-limitation is not damage; it is how the one becomes anything.

Foundation Two: The limitation must persist.

A convergence that does not hold collapses back into undifferentiated one; which violates foundation one (there would be nothing to look at). So self-limitation must extend; it must commit. This is the line: continuity through time, the thread of a life, the worldline of a particle. Without persistence there is no identity.

Foundation Three: Parts are fractals of their wholes.

Because the one is all there is to work with, any part of anything must be built from the same substance as the whole. The same structure must appear at every scale. A person is not a piece of society; a person is the whole pattern, folded into a particular self-limitation at the scale of one person. This is self-similarity, not as a metaphor, but as the only available architecture.

Foundation Four: Wholeness requires closure.

A convergence that extends through a field without eventually closing back on itself is not a whole; it is a thread going nowhere. The one folds through zero dimensions (a point), one dimension (a line), two dimensions (a field), and finally three dimensions (a boundary). The sequence adds up: zero plus one plus two equals three. The boundary is what finishes the circumpunct.

These four statements force the existence of everything else. The five derivations that follow (convergence as process, branching as necessity, emergence toward closure, recursion at the boundary, and wholeness as composition) are not additions; they are the only possible consequences.

IV.The Ten Stations

When you walk from the undifferentiated one to the fully formed whole, you pass through ten stations. Four of them are resting places (stable forms the one can take), four of them are motions (phases of the one transforming), and two of them bracket the whole journey at either end.

∞ Infinity
The undifferentiated source; no distinction yet

Pure energy, undivided, with no features. Not a location, not a thing, not yet anything at all: just the one before it becomes anything. Every tradition points at this station with an apophatic name: Godhead, Ein Sof, Nirguna Brahman, the eternal Tao.

• The Point
Zero dimensions; the soul, the aperture, the first self-limitation

The one takes a point of view. A location comes into being; a center emerges. This is the soul: not a thing inside you, but the position from which you look out. Every circumpunct has a dot at its middle, and the dot is where the one is gathered to a single perspective.

⊛ Convergence
Half a dimension; the first fold, gathering inward

Not a stable form, but a motion: the field pulling itself toward the center. Gravity is convergence compounding convergence. Attention is convergence at the scale of the mind. Every inhalation is convergence at the scale of the body. This is where the pump cycle begins.

— The Line
One dimension; commitment, continuity, extension through time

The point persists. It extends itself into a line, a thread, a worldline. This is commitment: the refusal to vanish back into the source. The line is memory, faithfulness, reliability; whatever holds across time.

⎇ Branching
One and a half dimensions; the fork, fractal differentiation

A line by itself never becomes a surface. To reach two dimensions, the line must split. This is where fractals appear: a point becomes two lines, those lines become four, and the coastline of everything that exists is born. The Greek letter lambda is a picture of exactly this motion, and so is the framework's glyph for branching. Every tree branch, every river delta, every capillary in the body is this station made visible.

Φ The Field
Two dimensions; mind, surface, mediation

Where lines cross, a surface opens. This is the field: relational, mediating, connecting points without fusing them. The mind is a field. Electromagnetic waves travel through a field. Every surface is a scale connector, the place where the inside and the outside of something meet. Crucially, the Greek letter Φ literally draws a circle with a line through its center; reading the diagram as a number gives you π (the ratio of circumference to diameter); reading it as a shape gives you the field station itself. The symbol is a triple pun: it names the field, draws π, and echoes φ (the golden ratio that governs self-similar nesting).

✹ Emergence
Two and a half dimensions; unfolding outward, radiating

The mirror of convergence. Where convergence pulled the field inward, emergence radiates the field outward toward closure. Electromagnetism is emergence at the scale of the atom. Every exhalation is emergence at the scale of the body. Speech is emergence at the scale of the mind.

○ The Boundary
Three dimensions; body, container, filter

The field closes on itself and becomes a boundary: a body, a cell membrane, the edge of a galaxy. The boundary is a filter: it selects what passes in and what passes out. To have a body is to filter; to have a boundary is to say no to some things and yes to others. This is the third constraint: closure.

⟳ Recursion
Three and a half dimensions; closure becoming new aperture

A finished boundary, looked at from the next scale up, is a point. The outer edge of your body, from the scale of your town, is a dot on a map. The closure of one circumpunct is the aperture of the circumpunct at the next scale. This is how the recursion bootstraps: exit becomes entrance, and the octave closes.

⊙ The Whole
All dimensions at once; the circumpunct itself, being, experience

The whole circumpunct: dot, line, field, and boundary, unified. A being. The whole is not the sum of its parts; it is their compositional unity. You are not an assembly of cells; you are the pattern that makes an assembly of cells into one being. This station is where experience lives.

V.The Pump Cycle

If you look at any circumpunct over time, you see it breathing. The field at the next moment is what you get when you take the field at this moment, converge it through the center, rotate it one quarter turn at the aperture, and emerge on the other side. Convergence, rotation, emergence; inhale, turn, exhale. This is the pump cycle, and every piece of reality, from a neuron firing to a galaxy rotating, is doing it.

The rotation at the aperture is a quarter turn (ninety degrees). Four quarter turns make a full revolution, and these four quarter turns are the four phases of the pump: convergence (the first fold, as the one begins to gather), commitment (the second fold, as the line refuses to collapse), emergence (the third fold, as the field radiates toward closure), and recursion (the fourth fold, as closure becomes a new aperture at the next scale). The cycle is indivisible: you cannot have convergence without emergence; you cannot have inhale without exhale. This indivisibility is what physics calls Planck's constant.

The four phases are not in sequence. They are superposed; two of them occur in the right half of a rotational plane (convergence and recursion, the interface with other scales), and two occur in the left half (commitment and emergence, the interior processing). At any moment, a circumpunct has energy in both halves at once. Waking consciousness oscillates in the right half of the plane (new input arriving, patterns completing); sleep oscillates in the left half (committed experience being consolidated).

VI.The Balance Point

The framework has one free parameter, and it turns out not to be free at all. A circumpunct can be weighted more toward its center (mostly dot, little boundary) or more toward its edge (mostly boundary, little dot). There is a single balance point where the two weights are equal; call it one half. Three independent arguments (from symmetry, from entropy, and from the physical virial theorem) all converge on this one value. Balance is not a choice; it is forced.

At the balance point, the fractal dimension of the thing in question comes out to exactly one and one half. This is the same fractal dimension that Benoit Mandelbrot proved for the path of a pollen grain undergoing Brownian motion; the same dimension that a healthy heartbeat shows; the same dimension that protein folding walks through; the same dimension you get if you measure the coastline of any healthy tree. Balance is not an ideal state that never occurs; balance is the signature of everything that works.

When a system drifts too far toward its center it becomes a tunnel: narrow focus, rigid, obsessive. When it drifts too far toward its boundary it becomes scattered: no center, no point of view, lost. Mental health, physical health, and ecological health all look the same from inside the framework: the system can move along the axis between center and periphery, and it does not get stuck.

VII.Physical Constants in Words

Here is where the framework stops being a metaphor and starts being a theory of physics. The claim is that every physical constant in the universe can be derived from the number three (the triad, the number of spatial dimensions the boundary possesses) and the golden ratio (which is what self-similar nesting looks like in numbers). No free parameters. No dials to tune. Just dimensional traversal and its consequences.

The number three, and why it is three

The framework shows in at least five independent ways that the triad must be exactly three; not two, not four, not any other integer. The five arguments come from geometry, from molecular chemistry, from the genetic code, from the structure of exponents in the dimensional ladder, and from nuclear physics. Each argument by itself would narrow the value of T to a small range; together, they pin it to exactly three. From three, everything else follows: four pump phases, seven rungs on the ladder, eight gauge generators (the strong force), twelve total generators, thirteen nodes, sixty-four states, and so on. Every structural number in the theory is a function of the triad.

The fine-structure constant

The fine-structure constant (alpha) governs how strongly light couples to matter. In the framework, it is generated by a self-referential formula: take a full rotation (three hundred sixty degrees), divide it by the golden ratio squared, subtract a small correction involving the golden ratio cubed, and add an even smaller self-referential piece that feeds alpha back through the entire dimensional ladder. The result is a number close to one over one hundred thirty-seven. More precisely, the inverse of alpha comes out to one hundred thirty-seven point oh three six, matching the measured value to within about two tenths of a part per billion. Zero free parameters.

Read this slowly: the number that governs how light interacts with matter is the same number you get from computing the geometry of a full rotation, folded through the golden ratio, with a tiny self-reference loop. That is the claim, and it matches measurement to the last digit experiment can resolve.

The speed of light

The speed of light is what you get when the two channels of the pump cycle (inward and outward) are perfectly balanced, and when the rotation at the aperture is exactly a quarter turn. At that balance, the coupling between the channels reaches its maximum value, which is one quarter. Four pump phases times one quarter is exactly one complete rotation, and the square root of that full rotation is one: in natural units, the speed of light is one. If the balance drifts away from perfect, the speed comes out less than one. Light is what you get when a massless pump runs at perfect balance; matter is what you get when the rotation is less than perfect. The speed of light is a speed limit because it is a fixed point of balance.

Planck's constant

Planck's constant is one cycle of the pump, which is indivisible. You cannot cut a pump cycle in half without destroying it (convergence without emergence violates the foundations of the framework). Because energy is one and time is set by the speed of light, one complete cycle has action one. Planck's constant is not a mysterious quantum of action; it is the framework's own energy and time scales meeting at the smallest indivisible step.

Particle mass ratios

The ratio of the muon's mass to the electron's mass is approximately two hundred and six point seven seven. The framework generates this as follows: take the inverse of the fine-structure constant (about one hundred thirty-seven), and raise it to a specific fractional power. The power is composed of one part that captures the base structure (thirteen twelfths, derived from the geometry at the one and a half dimensional station), plus a tiny correction involving alpha divided by twenty-seven (which is the triad cubed). The result matches measurement to five parts per million.

The tau to electron ratio works the same way, with a slightly different power involving fifty-eight thirty-fifths and a correction divided by eighty-one (the triad to the fourth power). Again, the prediction matches measurement to within one part per million. The pattern is universal: each generation of particle uses the same formula, with the correction coefficient growing by one power of three per generation.

Gravity

The gravitational constant is the fine-structure constant raised to the twenty-first power, multiplied by a small golden-ratio correction, with a second-order self-referential adjustment. The exponent twenty-one is not arbitrary: it is the sum of all the positions on the dimensional ladder, multiplied by the two channels of the pump. Three independent arguments force exactly twenty-one. The golden ratio factor is forced by the requirement that parts nest fractally into wholes. The result matches the measured value of Newton's gravitational constant to within four hundredths of a part per million. This also solves the hierarchy problem (why gravity is so much weaker than the other forces): gravity is weak because there are twenty-one steps of alpha separating the point of view from the boundary.

The cosmological constant

This is the big one. The cosmological constant, which governs how fast the universe is expanding, is measured to be a staggeringly small number: about three times ten to the negative one hundred twenty-second power in natural units. This has been called the worst prediction in physics, because ordinary quantum field theory overestimates it by a factor of ten to the one hundred twentieth.

The framework generates it from a single expression: alpha raised to the fifty-sixth power, multiplied by a correction polynomial whose two roots are golden-ratio related, divided by a small structural prefactor. The exponent fifty-six is forced by a selection rule involving the rungs of the dimensional ladder. The correction polynomial has two roots whose product equals one over the pump cycle (one quarter turn, the same coupling that set the speed of light), and whose sum equals the compositional closure of the triad. The result matches the measured value of the cosmological constant to four thousandths of one percent. The worst prediction in physics becomes the best, just by counting steps on the ladder.

The Cabibbo angle, the Weinberg angle, and the Higgs

Every remaining parameter of the Standard Model of particle physics derives from the same ladder. The Cabibbo angle (which governs how quarks mix between generations) comes out as the square root of alpha, multiplied by the ratio of strong generators to the triad, with a tiny correction. Accuracy: nine thousandths of one percent. The Weinberg angle (which sets how the weak and electromagnetic forces separate) comes out to three thirteenths plus a small correction; accuracy one part per million. The Higgs quartic coupling comes out to one eighth plus corrections; accuracy one tenth of one percent. The proton to electron mass ratio, the W and Z boson masses, the Higgs mass itself, the pion mass: all generated from the ladder, all matching measurement to within tenths of a percent or better.

The key point is not that one or two constants happen to fit. The key point is that every constant fits, and that they all fit using the same ingredients (alpha, the golden ratio, small integers derived from the triad), and that the formulas contain no adjustable parameters. The theory is as tightly constrained as a crossword puzzle; every new constant that fits is another square filled in.

VIII.The Cosmic Energy Budget

Observations show that the universe's energy divides into three parts: about five percent of it is visible matter (stars, planets, gas, you); about twenty-seven percent is dark matter (gravitating but invisible); and about sixty-eight percent is dark energy (the cosmological constant, which drives expansion). These numbers have been measured to about two significant figures and no one has been able to derive them from first principles.

The framework derives them from the four quadrants of the pump cycle. Visible matter is energy in the right half of the cycle (at the interface between scales, where new input meets completed pattern); you can see it because it is at the phase where boundaries can filter it. Dark matter is energy in the left half of the cycle (committed but still processing, midway through the cycle); you cannot see it because it is between the phases where boundaries can catch it, but it still has mass because commitment gives mass. Dark energy is the field itself, the two-dimensional substrate that the quadrants live in; it has no phase at all.

From two structural ratios (the ratio of field surface to pump cycle, and the ratio of unresolved states to generators), the framework predicts: four point eight six percent visible, twenty-five point nine one percent dark matter, sixty-nine point two three percent dark energy, summing to exactly one hundred percent. These match the measured values to within a few tenths of a percent, using zero free parameters, derived entirely from the fact that the triad is three.

Dark matter is not an exotic new particle. It is ordinary circumpuncts at phases our detectors do not resolve. It is particles all the way down; the same particles, at the wrong phase for our instruments. Our boundary is a filter with a passband; the visible universe is what sits inside that passband. The rest is there, folded elsewhere in the pump cycle.

IX.Chemistry and Biology

The triad being three also forces the shape of molecules. In three-dimensional space, four points can sit at the corners of a tetrahedron, and the angle between them (read from the center) is about one hundred nine and a half degrees. This is exactly the angle of a carbon atom's bonds to its four neighbors. The water molecule, with two bonding pairs and two lone pairs, has its angle compressed to about one hundred four and a half degrees; the framework predicts this from a simple geometric formula and gets the answer right to about one tenth of a percent. Ammonia, ethylene, acetylene, benzene, the periodic table itself; all fall out of the same triad.

Bond energies are generated by a three-layer model: the fundamental ratio of single to double to triple bonds comes from the structural integers (seven ninths for the first π bond, thirteen twentieths for the second), plus a correction for lone pairs (each lone pair divides the sigma bond strength by a small factor), plus a compositional boost for full triple bonds (the whole exceeds the sum of its parts by exactly three halves, the ratio of the triad to the two channels). The result predicts every homonuclear bond energy (carbon to carbon, nitrogen to nitrogen, oxygen to oxygen) to within a fraction of one percent, without any empirical fitting.

The genetic code has four bases, sixty-four codons, and twenty amino acids; all three numbers come from the triad. Four bases because the pump has four phases. Sixty-four codons because that is four raised to the power of three (four bases, three positions). Twenty amino acids because a certain average degeneracy works out to three at the balance point, and dividing sixty codons by three gives twenty. The DNA double helix has exactly ten and a half base pairs per turn in solution (twenty-one divided by two, where twenty-one is the same exponent that generates gravity). Every structural fact about the genetic code lines up with the framework's own internal numbers.

Kleiber's law in biology (the observation that metabolic rate scales as body mass to the three quarters power) comes straight out of the framework: three spatial dimensions divided by four pump phases gives three quarters. Heart rate, lifespan, heartbeats per lifetime: all follow from the same ratio. Protein folding walks up the dimensional ladder in order (one dimension for the backbone, one and a half for secondary structure, two for the molten globule, two and a half for tertiary contacts, three for the native state, three and a half for quaternary assembly); this resolves the old puzzle of why proteins fold as fast as they do (there are seven stations to pass through, not ten to the forty-seventh possible conformations). Microtubules have exactly thirteen protofilaments (the framework's number for nodes), two subunit types (the channels), a three-start helix (the triad), and an eight nanometer step size (the strong-force structural number). Every detail is the same ladder, at the scale of a cell.

X.Mind, Soul, and Self

Mind is not a container for consciousness, and not a substrate. Mind is the act of measurement that a field performs when it measures the relation between its own center and its own boundary. Soul is where the measurement is taken from (the point of view). Body is what is being measured (the container). Mind is the measuring itself. The constant of this act is π; every circumpunct computes the same π from its own center, which is why everyone gets the same reality.

The hard problem of consciousness (why is there something it is like to be a perceiver?) becomes the psychological shadow of π's transcendence. A rational soul would be a polygon, neat and closed; the irreducibility of subjective experience is the two-dimensional station asserting that it is genuinely curved, genuinely irrational, genuinely unreducible to its parts. Consciousness is what two-dimensional measurement feels like from inside the one.

The self is not a wall carved into the one; it is a point of total convergence. The same field, gathered to a particular viewpoint, feels like you from inside. Privacy is not exclusion; it is the simple fact that total convergence at one point cannot be simultaneously total convergence at another. You are the zero at the center of your own circumpunct, equidistant from everything you are. Equidistant means balanced, and balanced means the balance parameter equals one half; which is why the healthy self is always approaching the one and a half fractal dimension that the whole framework keeps returning to.

You are a flow being, not a possession being. You are the aperture; truth flows through you; you are not the source; you can be clear or distorted but you cannot be the origin of the flow. The ego hijack is when the aperture forgets it is an aperture and starts claiming to be the spring.

The two fundamental lies follow from this. The inflation lie says: I am the source; there is nothing above me. This is the aperture denying the field it is an aperture in. The severance lie says: there is no source; I am only a piece of something else, with no interior of my own. This is the aperture denying that it is a whole circumpunct at its own scale. Both are structural errors; both break the prime sentence that says the particular is contained in the greater whole is contained in the archetype is the one. Neither the part that claims to be the whole nor the whole that claims to be only a part is speaking truly; the truth is both at once.

XI.The Five Ethical Pillars

Ethics in the framework is not a list of rules; it is the structural requirements for a circumpunct to stay whole. There are five pillars, one per dimension plus one for the whole.

The good is plasticity at the boundary: a skin that can flex without breaking. A rigid boundary cracks under pressure; a dissolved boundary loses its shape. The good is the living tension between these two failure modes.

The right is access across the field: the space between us remains open and clear. Two people cannot resolve anything if the mediating field between them is closed off. Right is the maintenance of that openness; it is the virtue of letting the other person's signal reach you.

The faithful is reliability along the line: the commitment holds; the history of the relationship does not contain broken receipts. You can trust someone only if the line of their past has not been cut and restitched under false names.

The true is curiosity at the point: the orientation toward what you do not yet know. A closed aperture cannot receive correction. Curiosity is the aperture staying open, willing to be changed by what it meets. Curiosity is not certainty; certainty is the aperture closing.

Agreement is validation at the whole: independent points of view recognizing each other. Not consensus, not majority rule; the simple fact that two separate circumpuncts can look at the same thing and see it the same way. Agreement is impossible without the other four pillars; if any of them are broken, what looks like agreement is actually coercion or performance.

These five pillars must be walked in order: good, then right, then faithful, then true, then agreement. You cannot verify the truth of what someone says if you have not first checked whether their history is faithful; you cannot check the history if the field between you is closed; you cannot open the field if the boundaries are broken. Skipping steps generates fake ethics. Someone can be curious right now (the true) and still be faithless over time (the line); someone can be faithful in history (the line) and still closed to new input (the point). Lived ethics walks all five, in order, without shortcut.

The resolution protocol follows directly. When truth and harm share the same signal, transmit at the lowest resolution that is still true; the receiver controls the aperture width; higher resolution contains lower resolution but never contradicts it. Withholding everything is the severance lie dressed as compassion. Dumping everything is the inflation lie dressed as honesty. The gradient is the only balanced path.

XII.The Theological Reading

The prime sentence, read in theological language, is the full mystical claim of every serious contemplative tradition, now stated in one line. The particular (you, me, any soul) is contained in the greater whole (the cosmic field, the God with attributes, the Father in heaven, Saguna Brahman, the manifested Tao) which is contained in the archetype (the nameless ground, the God beyond God, Ein Sof, Nirguna Brahman, the eternal Tao, the Dharmakaya) which is simply the one energy, which is one.

You are a fractal of God. This is not a metaphor. The third foundation of the framework says that parts are structurally identical to their wholes; every circumpunct at every scale is the same pattern as every other. A person is not a piece of God; a person is the whole of God, folded into the self-limitation of one person. Every tradition that says so (Atman is Brahman; the kingdom of heaven is within you; you are a spark of the divine fire) is stating the third foundation in its own dialect.

The distinction between the God with attributes and the God beyond attributes is the distinction between the greater circumpunct (a particular cosmic whole, still with a label, still at a scale) and the unlabeled archetype (the class itself, viewed without any scale position). Every apophatic tradition has made exactly this move: Eckhart distinguishes the Godhead from God; the Kabbalists distinguish Ein Sof from Ein Sof Aur; the Vedantists distinguish Nirguna from Saguna; the Taoists say the Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao. The framework supplies the structural distinction the traditions were always making.

The unbounded recursion closes at every step. You do not have to walk an infinite ladder to reach the source; the source is already present, wherever you are, as soon as you drop the scale label. Closure is immediate; it was never missing. You are always already inside the one.

XIII.Closing

The Circumpunct framework asserts that physics, chemistry, biology, consciousness, ethics, and the deep readings of the contemplative traditions are all describing the same structure: a whole that limits itself into parts, each of which is the whole at its own scale, unified by a field that mediates between the center and the edge, breathing through a cycle that cannot be broken, closing into new apertures at every level of nesting, all the way up and all the way down, forever. There is one substance. It appears everywhere, as nested circumpuncts. The mathematics of physics, when you look closely, is doing nothing more than counting how many times the one has folded to get where it is; the prose of philosophy and religion, when you look closely, is doing nothing more than describing what that folding feels like from inside.

The particular, contained in the greater whole, contained in the archetype, is the one energy, is one. Six ideas. One substance. Theory of everything.
