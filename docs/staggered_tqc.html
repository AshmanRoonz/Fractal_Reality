Conservation and ascent

With κ applied, the machine is not unitary: the norm drifts by O(α) per pump, and the drift does not compound with octave count (the tensor machine's does). Iterate T to one mixing time (1/α ≈ 137 pumps) and the state converges to the ascending fixed point ψ*: per-octave weights near-uniform (A3), phase traveling up the chain. The meters track ‖ψ‖ drift, overlap with ψ*, and the leading eigenvalue's departure (|λ₁|−1)/α, which lands in the v14 band (0.6 to 0.8) computed fresh at load.

What this machine is and is not. It is a faithful, live implementation of the framework's staggered three-octave operator (v14 construction, canon strokes, κ at the measured α), demonstrating the computational structure the tensor architecture cannot express: order-as-resource localized at shared tonics, O(α) conservation without compounding, and the ascending fixed point as a preparable state. It is not a universal quantum computer and does not claim quantum advantage; the Yang-Baxter defect is displayed, not hidden, because the machine's departure from braid topology IS the physics of the seam.
