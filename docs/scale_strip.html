§1 — Premise

Each particle of matter is a circumpunct ⊙ₙ at some scale n. By Definition 1.3, its boundary ○ₙ is composed of circumpuncts at scale n−1:

○ₙ = { ⊙ₓ⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾ | x ∈ Σₙ }

The states of matter — solid, liquid, gas, plasma — are not properties of individual particles. They are collective regimes of a population of circumpuncts, determined by two relational parameters:

Definition 1 — State Parameters

For a population of circumpuncts { ⊙ₙ } at scale n:

  • v — velocity (kinetic energy per particle). The rate at which each •ₙ drives its convergence-emergence cycle. Aperture activity.
  • d — mean spacing between boundaries. The field gap: how much Φ mediates between adjacent ○ₙ.

The state of the collective is a function of both. High velocity with tight spacing produces different regimes than high velocity with wide spacing. Both parameters are needed.

Remark — The Plasma Threshold

The solid → liquid → gas transitions modify the relationships between intact circumpuncts. Spacing and velocity change, but each ⊙ₙ retains its boundary.

Plasma is qualitatively different. It is the transition where ○ₙ itself fails — where the energy exceeds the binding capacity of the boundary. This is not another relational shift between peers. It is the dissolution of a scale level.

§3 — Scale Strip

Definition 2 — Scale Strip (𝒮↓)

A scale strip is an energy-driven operation that dissolves the boundary ○ₙ of circumpuncts at scale n, liberating the sub-scale circumpuncts ⊙ₙ₋₁ that composed it.

𝒮↓ : ⊙ₙ → { ⊙ₓ⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾ | x ∈ Σₙ }
The boundary scatters into its constituent apertures.

Precondition: Mean kinetic energy per particle exceeds the binding energy of ○ₙ:

⟨E_kin⟩ > E_bind(○ₙ)

Result: The population transitions from N circumpuncts at scale n to a larger population of circumpuncts at scale n−1, interacting through field Φ without the organizational constraint of ○ₙ.

Properties

Proposition 3.1 — Nothing Is Destroyed

𝒮↓ does not annihilate the sub-scale circumpuncts. They were always present as the boundary (§2.1.1: the boundary aperture measure μ_○). The strip operation releases them from their role as someone else's boundary and allows them to operate as independent ⊙.

∎

Proposition 3.2 — Scale Strip Cascades

𝒮↓ can be applied iteratively. Each application peels one level of the fractal hierarchy:

⊙_atom — scale n (atomic matter) → 𝒮↓ ionization ⊙_ion + ⊙_electron — scale n−1 (plasma) → 𝒮↓ nuclear dissociation ⊙_nucleon — scale n−2 (nucleon gas) → 𝒮↓ deconfinement ⊙_quark + ⊙_gluon — scale n−3 (quark-gluon plasma)

Each step requires exponentially more energy (E_bind grows with depth) and exposes a deeper layer of the ⊙ hierarchy.

Proposition 3.3 — Field Governance Increases With Each Strip

At each stripped level, the boundary's organizational role diminishes and Φ takes over. In quark-gluon plasma, there is no hadronic boundary — the strong field itself (color force) is the sole organizing principle. The sequence solid → plasma → QGP is a monotonic transfer of governance from ○ to Φ.

§4 — Scale Wrap

Definition 3 — Scale Wrap (𝒮↑)

A scale wrap is an energy-dissipation operation in which circumpuncts at scale n−1 compose themselves into a new boundary ○ₙ, forming circumpuncts at scale n.

𝒮↑ : { ⊙ₓ⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾ } → ⊙ₙ
Free apertures compose into a new boundary. Structure accretes.

Precondition: Mean kinetic energy drops below the binding threshold of ○ₙ. The field configuration permits coherent boundary formation:

⟨E_kin⟩ < E_bind(○ₙ) ∧ T(Δφ) > T_crit
Energy low enough AND phase coherence high enough for boundary to hold.

Properties

Proposition 4.1 — Wrap Is a Ratchet

Each scale wrap is a ratchet operation (§2.7). Once ○ₙ forms, the system cannot freely reverse — it takes energy input ≥ E_bind(○ₙ) to strip again. The wrap breaks detailed balance: the rate of formation exceeds the rate of dissolution at sub-threshold energies.

k(𝒮↑) > k(𝒮↓) when ⟨E_kin⟩ < E_bind(○ₙ)
Proposition 4.2 — The Cosmological Wrap Cascade

The thermal history of the universe is a sequence of scale wraps as mean energy density drops:

⊙_quark + ⊙_gluon — QGP (T ~ 10¹² K) → 𝒮↑ hadronization ⊙_nucleon — hadronic matter → 𝒮↑ nucleosynthesis (T ~ 10⁹ K) ⊙_nucleus — light nuclei → 𝒮↑ recombination (T ~ 3000 K) ⊙_atom — neutral atoms (CMB released) → 𝒮↑ molecular formation, condensation ⊙_molecule → ⊙_crystal → ⊙_cell → ...

Each wrap adds a new ○ to the hierarchy. Each new ○ is made of ⊙ at the previous scale. The universe builds itself by recursive boundary formation.

𝒮↓ and 𝒮↑ are inverse operations on the nesting depth n of the circumpunct hierarchy:

𝒮↑ ∘ 𝒮↓ [⊙ₙ] ≅ ⊙ₙ

  5. ∴ Structural isomorphism. Not identity.

∎

§6 — Plasma as the First Scale Strip

Plasma is the first 𝒮↓ accessible at ordinary energies. It is what happens when atomic-scale circumpuncts lose their boundary:

𝒮↓(⊙_atom) → ⊙_ion + ⊙_electron
The electron was always part of ○_atom. Plasma frees it.
Remark — Plasma Reveals What Boundary Conceals

In intact matter, the field dynamics of ⊙ₙ₋₁ are hidden inside ○ₙ. You cannot see the electromagnetic field structure because the boundary is doing its job — containing, separating, defining.

Plasma strips ○. Now the Φ dynamics become macroscopically visible: magnetic field lines, plasma oscillations, Debye shielding, pinch effects. These are all field-mediated phenomena that were always present, now exposed by the removal of boundary.

Plasma does not create new physics. It reveals the physics that boundary was masking.

The sun is plasma throughout. It is a circumpunct whose interior is entirely scale-stripped — boundary dissolved, field governing — while its outer surface reconstitutes just enough ○ to define an interface with the cosmos.
