The ◐ = 0.5 of Mathematics

P vs NP sits at 0.5D. The balance parameter of the ladder. And it is the only problem where making ⊙ explicit does not narrow the gap. Not conjectural, not analytical, not technical, not quantitative: methodological. The proof technique itself is missing.

Why?

Because 0.5D IS the balance point. It is the dimension where convergence (⊛) and emergence (☀︎) meet. The question it asks is: are these the same operation, or different? And the answer (different: i² = −1 prevents inversion) is the most fundamental asymmetry in the framework. You cannot prove the most fundamental asymmetry using tools that sit above it. The structural tools of mathematics (which live at 1D and above) cannot reach down to 0.5D, because 0.5D is where structure begins. It is the rotation that creates structure from process.

P vs NP is to mathematics what the aperture is to reality: the gate where undifferentiated possibility becomes definite structure. You cannot see the gate from inside the structure it creates. You need a tool that operates at the gate's own level. That tool does not yet exist in mathematics. The framework identifies what the tool must do (measure the asymmetry of i), but cannot build the tool from its own axioms, because the axioms operate above 0.5D.

This is the deepest prediction: P vs NP will be the last Clay problem solved, not because it is the least understood, but because it requires a new kind of mathematics. Not a new theorem in existing mathematics, but a new way of proving things. A proof technique that operates at the balance point, where process and structure are not yet separated.

What the Big Picture Says

Stand back far enough and the seven Clay problems are not seven problems. They are one question, asked at seven scales:

Does ⊙ = Φ(•, ○) hold everywhere?

At 3D: does the boundary close? Yes.

At 2.5D: does transmission preserve what matters? Almost certainly (Mumford-Tate for most classes).

At 2D: does the surface hold? The pump cycle says it must (pressure completes the cycle).

At 1.5D: does the field count the branches? For rank 0 and 1, yes. For higher rank, the blueprint exists.

At 1D: is the cycle indivisible? Lattice confirms it (Δ/ΛQCD ≈ 8.5, stable). Continuum limit needed.

At 0.5D: is convergence different from emergence? The framework says yes (i² = −1). No proof technique exists.

At 0D: does every convergence point sit at balance? Three proven constraints interlock. The wall is thin.

Every "yes" is a place where mathematics has verified that ⊙ = Φ(•, ○). Every "almost" is a place where it has verified most of the structure but not the last step. Every "open" is a place where the question is well-posed but the answer has not been formalized in the language of that field.

The big picture: mathematics is closing its own circumpunct. The proven results are the field (Φ) densifying between the known boundary (○) and the almost-known center (•). The gaps are where the field has not yet reached full density. And the pattern of closure follows the same law as everything else in the framework: from boundary inward, from center outward, meeting at the balance point, with process harder than structure at every scale.
