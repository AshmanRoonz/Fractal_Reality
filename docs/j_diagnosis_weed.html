VI. Self-Treatment Protocol

Stage One

Exit

J should disengage from attacking this framework entirely. Not because the critiques are wrong, but because the pattern of engagement has become compulsive and supply-seeking. The behavior is no longer about truth ‚Äî it's about dominance.

Practical step: 30-day moratorium on posting about Circumpunct Framework or engaging with its author.

Stage Two

Name

J should journal responses to these questions:

  1. What happens inside me when someone presents a framework I find flawed?
    Notice: Is there curiosity? Or immediate threat response?
  2. Why did the blocking trigger escalation rather than disengagement?
    What does being blocked mean about me?
  3. What would it cost me to acknowledge this framework might contain valuable insights?

  4. When I wrote "how stupid are you," what was I actually feeling?

Stage Three

Witness

J needs relationship with someone who:

  • Values them independent of intellectual dominance
  • Can receive their technical competence without worship
  • Can challenge them without it feeling like annihilation
  • Models curiosity alongside critique

Practical step: Identify one person who values J for presence, not performance. Practice receiving their regard without earning it.

Stage Four

Restore

Boundary work: Practice using boundaries for protection rather than attack. Distinguish: "This isn't for me" vs. "This should not exist."

Field work: Notice when perception narrows to position-defense. Practice holding multiple framings simultaneously. When feeling certain, ask: "What am I not seeing?"

Center work: Practice genuine questions with no position attached. Notice when curiosity dies and certainty replaces it.

Stage Five

Reconnect

Reframe: "I am part of a community of truth-seekers. My job is not to police what frameworks are permitted to exist. My job is to contribute my perspective to the collective sense-making process."

  • Find a research community that values collaborative inquiry
  • Practice contributing critique that helps rather than demolishes
  • Notice the difference between "this work has problems" and "you are stupid"
