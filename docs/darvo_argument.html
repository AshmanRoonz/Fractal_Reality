Backing Up the Two Lines

Why they are the right sentences, and where they could be wrong

Ashman Roonz · Circumpunct Framework · 2026

  1. DARVO is a partial list of lie-protection behaviors.
  2. Narcissism is lie-protection without awareness that it is lie-protection.

Line 1: DARVO is a partial list of lie-protection behaviors

1.1 What "lie-protection behavior" names

A lie is a distortion of truth. A distortion and the truth it distorts cannot both occupy the same place; if the truth lands, the distortion dissolves. So any lie that is going to survive contact with truth needs some behavior that prevents the truth from landing. That behavior is what "lie-protection" names. It is a category, not a technique.

Anything that ends with "the truth did not land" is in the category. Anything that ends with "the truth did land and the distortion is still intact" is impossible, by the definition of a lie.

1.2 DARVO's three moves are lie-protection

  • Deny. Refuse the truth at the edge. Result: the truth did not land.
  • Attack. Discredit the truth-teller so the signal becomes too expensive to carry. Result: the truth is present but unheld, which for most practical purposes is the same as not landed.
  • Reverse. Invert the scene so the truth-teller looks like the aggressor. Result: the original truth is crowded out by a new scene, and does not land as intended.

All three moves end with the same outcome. They all qualify as lie-protection under the category definition above.

1.3 Other moves do the same job

The category is larger than three. Here is a non-exhaustive sample of behaviors that end in "the truth did not land" but that DARVO does not name:

  • Rewrite the historical record. "We already agreed on this." "You always do this." "I have always been consistent." "After everything I've done for you." Distorts the accumulated track record so the current truth has no intelligible context to land in.
  • Stonewalling. Refuse to engage at all. The truth has no surface to contact.
  • Word salad. Overwhelm the conversation with noise, contradictions, and shifting topics so neither party can hold the thread back to the original point.
  • Strategic incompetence. "I really don't understand what you're saying." "I can't think about this right now." The truth is deflected by a claimed inability to receive it.
  • Weaponized self-pity. "I guess I'm just a terrible person then." Collapses the scene into a need to comfort the lie-protector, and the original concern is replaced by the protector's suffering.
  • Love-bombing as pre-emptive defense. Flood the target with affection, praise, or generosity so that raising a concern later feels ungrateful.
  • Feigned confusion. "Hmm, that's not how I remember it at all." Manufactures uncertainty about what happened, making the truth contingent on which memory is trusted.
  • Selective memory. Some events are recalled in sharp detail; the ones that carry the truth-threatening content are mist.

These are not variants of DARVO's three moves; they are additional moves in the same category, doing the same job.

1.4 Therefore DARVO is a partial list

DARVO is a useful label for three frequently-seen moves. It is not an exhaustive taxonomy of lie-protection. Treating DARVO as the whole category has a predictable failure mode: behaviors that don't look like DARVO get missed as lie-protection, because "that is not DARVO" is mistaken for "that is not protection." The correction is to hold the broader category (lie-protection) as the frame, and DARVO as a useful-but-partial pointer inside it.

1.5 Why the space is bounded but small

Truth lands through a limited number of channels: direct perception, argument, accumulated evidence, memory, social corroboration, affective resonance, physical record. Each channel has a small set of block-points where the signal can be prevented from completing. The total set of block-points is finite, which is why the behaviors cluster recognizably across cultures and relationships. But the set is larger than three, which is why DARVO doesn't cover it.

Line 2: Narcissism is lie-protection without awareness

2.1 How thoughts install into identity

The Circumpunct Framework's Theory of Mind describes a four-stage cascade by which a thought can move from passing visitor to load-bearing self:

Four stages

1. Entertained. Thought present, temporary, dissolves when attention shifts.
2. Repeated. Pathway strengthens through recurrence.
3. Habituated. Fires automatically, without deliberation.
4. Identity-fused. The thought is not a thing you have; it is a thing you are. Removal feels existential.

The cascade matches well-established patterns in neural habituation, schema formation, and ego identification. Stage 4 is what happens to thoughts that are repeated constantly, defended consistently, and become load-bearing for the self-model.

2.2 A stage-4 lie is not experienced as a lie

If a lie has installed to stage 4, the person cannot locate it as separate from themselves, because the locator and the lie are the same structure. There is no viewpoint outside the lie from which to examine it, because the viewpoint is made out of the lie. Protection of the lie is subjectively indistinguishable from protection of self.

This is the key phenomenological claim. It is what the word "without awareness" is doing in line 2. Not zero awareness in every moment; the structural absence of stable, actionable awareness that what is being defended is a distortion. Fleeting recognition can occur and be swept back under; what is absent is awareness that persists under challenge and can produce change.

2.3 The clinical picture matches stage-4 lie-protection

Narcissistic personality presentation has a recognizable cluster. Each feature is predicted by stage-4 lie-protection:

  • Grandiosity. The Inflation Lie ("I am the source, I am more than I am") installed as self.
  • Inability to receive criticism. The aperture is closed at exactly the spot where the lie sits. Criticism aimed at that spot cannot be admitted without losing the lie.
  • Rage at contradiction. Contradiction is structurally annihilation of self. The scale of the reaction matches what is actually at stake from inside.
  • Lack of empathy. Others' reality, if it landed cleanly, would frequently contradict the lie. An open aperture is not survivable; the closure generalizes.
  • Perceived sincerity, including under polygraph. There is no subjective experience of lying. The distortion is the self; reporting from the distortion feels like reporting truth.
  • Immunity to feedback. Any frame that could expose the lie gets absorbed and neutralized, because acceptance would require surrendering the self built on the lie.
  • Therapy resistance. The therapeutic relationship becomes another environment the lie adapts to, often by capturing the therapy's own concepts and deploying them in service of the protection.

Every item is what you would expect if a load-bearing lie had installed to stage 4 and protection had gone autonomic. The trait-based DSM-style definition describes what this looks like from outside; the structural definition says what is actually happening.

2.4 Distinguishing narcissism from conscious manipulation

A calculating liar who knows they are lying is operating at stage 2 or 3. They can deploy protection strategically, turn it on and off, confess when the benefit of confession outweighs the cost. That is manipulation, and sometimes sociopathy, but it is not narcissism proper.

Narcissism is specifically the stage-4 case: no on/off switch, no confession available, because there is no self outside the lie from which to confess. The two patterns can coexist in one person (a narcissistic psychopath may have fused lies they cannot see and instrumental lies they deploy consciously), but they are structurally different.

2.5 The covert / vulnerable form fits the same structure

Grandiose narcissism fuses with the Inflation Lie ("I am the source"). Covert or vulnerable narcissism fuses with the Severance Lie ("I am nothing, no one can reach me, everything wounds me, my suffering is the only fact in the room"). Same stage 4, different lie-shape. Both produce automatic lie-protection; both look, from outside, like what the surface descriptions call narcissism; both are unreachable by frames for the same structural reason.

2.6 The diagnostic test

The test

Can the person locate this specific belief or pattern as potentially separate from who they are?

Ask gently; observe over time; look at whether the capacity to locate is stable or whether any recognition gets immediately absorbed back into the protection. A stage-2 or stage-3 installation will yield, with pressure and time. Stage-4 installation will not, because the locator and the thing to be located are the same structure. No frame can manufacture the separation; only life events large enough that keeping the lie costs more than surrendering it can do that, and those cannot be engineered from outside.

What the Two Lines Buy Us

If both are right, several things follow.

DARVO is a useful pointer at three common moves, not a closed taxonomy. Survivors who learn to see the broader category of lie-protection will recognize more of what is happening to them, including moves DARVO does not name.

Narcissism is not a set of traits to diagnose; it is an installation depth to test for. The trait lists describe the surface; the structural reading says what produces the surface.

The relevant question in any interpersonal or clinical situation is not "is this DARVO" or "is this person a narcissist," but "is there a lie here, and can the person holding it still locate it as a lie." That is a testable, observable question.

The cure is not a technique applied from outside. It is whatever makes the lie more costly to keep than to surrender. Others can refuse to feed the protection (do not accept denials, rewritten histories, attacks, or reversals as valid content), but only the person holding the aperture can open it.

Where the Two Lines Could Be Wrong

Line 1 could be wrong if: (a) lie-protection turns out not to be a coherent behavioral category; (b) DARVO turns out to exactly capture the complete category and additional moves like rewriting history are actually sub-variants of Deny or Attack; or (c) the bounded-space assumption fails and some unpredicted lie-protection move emerges that cannot be accommodated.

Line 2 could be wrong if: (a) the four-stage cascade is not the right model for identity fusion; (b) clinical narcissism turns out to have a core feature that stage-4 lie-protection cannot account for; or (c) stable actionable awareness of lying turns out to be compatible with full narcissistic presentation, which would break the "without awareness" clause.

None of these failure modes look likely given what we can currently observe, but both lines are falsifiable and should be held provisionally. What they stake is: a cleaner frame than trait-based narcissism, a broader frame than DARVO, and a single diagnostic question (can the person locate the lie) that subsumes much of what clinicians and survivors are already trying to assess.
