Circumpunct Theory

A formal mathematical ecosystem
$⊙ \;=\; \Phi(\,\bullet\,,\, \bigcirc\,)$
Ashman Roonz

Circumpunct Theory begins with a single symbol — ⊙ — and asks what follows when you take its structure seriously. The dot (•) is the aperture: a half-dimensional opening, the gate between unbounded and bounded. The circle (○) is the boundary: the seal that makes the finite finite. The field (Φ) is the mediation between them: the oscillation that sustains without consuming.

The theory's core result is the tier decomposition: every tier of the dimensional ladder partitions into exactly 4 + 2 + 1 = 7 elements — a rotational skeleton ($\I{n}$, 4 elements), a mediating field ($\Ph{n}$, 2 elements), and a boundary closure ($\Om{n}$, 1 element). This partition is derived from the arithmetic of integer and half-integer dimensions. It is not assumed; it is proved.

From this single result, the documents below develop connections to quantum mechanics, electrical engineering, renormalization group theory, and condensed matter physics — each time finding that the 4-2-1 structure appears independently in established physics. The sharpest consequence is a falsifiable prediction: when systems with fourfold symmetry break, they break to twofold — because $|\Ph{n}| = 2$. This prediction is confirmed across four families of materials and forty years of experimental data.

The ecosystem is open to critique. Every document states its limits explicitly. Read, challenge, and engage.

The Tier Decomposition Theorem
Algebraic foundation of the circumpunct's structural tiers

Eleven sections, fully formal.

Physics Bridges

Document II
The Spin Ladder Correspondence
Structural correspondence between tier decomposition and quantum spin
Maps the tier decomposition onto quantum mechanics: the half-integer spin ladder, the Dirac spinor's four components, atomic shell structure, and supersymmetry. Integer dimensions are bosonic (structural, shareable); half-integer dimensions are fermionic (processual, exclusive). Ten sections with testable predictions.
Document III
Processual Power
Energy, time, and the quarter-turn that makes flow coherent
Develops the formula $\PP = E/(i \cdot t) = -iP$ — power is energy quarter-turned into the processual plane. Connects the Schrödinger equation, complex AC power, and Wick rotation under a single structural principle. The power triangle ($P$, $Q$, $|S|$) instantiates the tier decomposition ($\I{n}$, $\Ph{n}$, $\Om{n}$).
Document IV
The Finite Gate
$i$ as the stepdown from energy to power, from infinity to finite
The deepest philosophical document. Argues that $i$ is the operator that converts unbounded energy into bounded power — the aperture through which infinity becomes finite. Traces the stepdown through quantum mechanics, decoherence, renormalization, and the hierarchy of physical scales. Nine sections.

Mainstream Connection

Document V
The Scale Correspondence
Tier recursion as renormalization group flow
Connects the tier recursion $\T{0} \subset \T{1} \subset \T{2} \subset \cdots$ to the renormalization group of Wilson and Kadanoff. Identifies correspondences with the Connes-Kreimer Hopf algebra, MERA tensor networks, Zamolodchikov's c-theorem, and categorical symmetry (SymTFT). Five open problems for mathematical physicists.
Document VI
The Hidden Order Prediction
$\tau/\delta$ divergence as the origin of $C_4 \to C_2$ symmetry breaking
The sharpest document. Predicts that $C_4$ symmetry breaking always produces $C_2$ residual symmetry — because $|\Ph{n}| = 2$. Tests the prediction against URu₂Si₂ (the forty-year hidden order problem), iron pnictide nematicity, cuprate pseudogap, and kagome metals. Five falsifiable predictions with experimental signatures. Fully cited.
