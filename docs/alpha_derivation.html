2. The base formula

\[ \frac{1}{\alpha_0} \;=\; \frac{360}{\varphi^2} \;-\; \frac{2}{\varphi^3}. \]

Symbol 1: 360

The numerator 360 is the integer count of structural units in one complete pump cycle. The pump cycle is four beats (P = 4), each beat a primitive cycle-generator (i-stroke, quarter-turn); cycle closure forces \(i^4 = 1\). The cycle's integer content factors on the ladder as:

\[ 360 \;=\; P! \,\cdot\, T \,\cdot\, (\Phi + \circ) \;=\; 24 \,\cdot\, 3 \,\cdot\, 5 \]

  • \(T = 3\): triad of structural stations traversed (count type)
  • \(\Phi + \circ\): dimensional sum, 2D + 3D, which by the Octave-Wrap Lemma lands at 1.5D' (branching at the next scale). The lemma supplies the type-consistency argument that distinguishes this from alternative pool-native integer additions (e.g., \(\Phi + T = 5\) is type-illegal: dimensional + count). Without the lemma, the \((\Phi + \circ)\) piece is an uninterpreted integer; with the lemma, it reads as "⊙λ's completed higher-D content participating in ⊙Λ's differentiation."

Note on units: 360 enters the formula as a pure integer, not as a degree-measure. The equation \(1/\alpha = 360/\varphi^2 - 2/\varphi^3 + \alpha/(59/3)\) is dimensionless on both sides; no term carries an angular unit. "One full pump cycle" is a geometric interpretation of the integer 360 (four quarter-turns compose a turn), not the source of the integer. The integer source is the ladder factorization \(P! \cdot T \cdot (\Phi + \circ)\), which is pool-native under type-consistent composition. 3. The self-referential closure

The \(2.7\) ppm residual is not random noise. The framework predicts it arises from \(\alpha\) feeding back into its own determination through the full dimensional ladder. The closed form:

\[ \frac{1}{\alpha} \;=\; \frac{360}{\varphi^2} \,-\, \frac{2}{\varphi^3} \,+\, \frac{\alpha}{21 - \tfrac{4}{3}} \]

The correction term \(\alpha / (21 - 4/3)\) has three independent structural constraints; each rules out wide classes of alternatives. All three must be satisfied simultaneously, and together they determine the term uniquely.

Constraint 1: First order in \(\alpha\)

The Kernel (the pump cycle) executes one complete cycle per scale; its indivisibility is \(\hbar = 1\). One feedback loop equals one power of \(\alpha\). Higher powers (\(\alpha^2\), \(\alpha^3\)) belong to higher-dimensional stations: \(\alpha^2\) appears in the Higgs quartic (2D, §27.7i), the cosmological constant correction (2D, §27.7g), and the gravitational coupling (boundary, §27.7). At 0D the correction is first-order.

Rules out: \(\alpha^2 / k\), \(\alpha^{3/2} / k\), higher powers.

Constraint 2: Additive, not multiplicative

The base term and the correction both contribute at the same dimensional station (0D). The accumulated traversal function \(A(d) = d(2d+1)\) is additive within a station; quantities at the same station add, quantities across stations compose multiplicatively. Therefore \(1/\alpha = \text{base} + \text{correction}\), not \(1/\alpha = \text{base} \times \text{correction}\).

Rules out: \(1/\alpha = \text{base} \cdot (1 + \alpha / k)\), \(1/\alpha = \text{base} \cdot \alpha / k\).

Constraint 3: Denominator \(= 59/3\) from conservation at the formula level

The total traversal budget at closure is \(A(3) = 3 \cdot (2 \cdot 3 + 1) = 21\). The base term already consumed the process-to-structure ratio \(P/T = 4/3\) of this budget (P counts process beats, T counts structural stations visited; the same ratio that gives Kleiber's \(T/P = 3/4\) in biology). \[ k \;=\; A(3) \,-\, P/T \;=\; 21 \,-\, \tfrac{4}{3} \;=\; \tfrac{59}{3}. \]

This applies A4 (conservation) at the formula level, justified by A3: if conservation holds at the dimensional level (0+1+2=3), at the exponent level (\(E(3) = 21\)), and at every physical scale (A3), then it holds at the formula level too, because a formula is a ⊙ at its own scale.

7. Falsification

What would break this derivation
  1. A CODATA measurement of \(1/\alpha\) outside \(137.035999147 \pm 1\) ppb. The current residual is 0.22 ppb at 1.4σ. Future precision measurements that push \(1/\alpha\) to, say, 137.036000 ± 10⁻¹¹ would put the prediction out of reach; the formula has no tuning to accommodate this.
  2. A shift in any of the nine cross-validation constants that forces a different integer pool. If a new precision measurement of \(m_\mu/m_e\), \(\sin^2\theta_W\), or \(G\) required replacing 21, 13, or 59/3 with a different ratio, the \(\alpha\) derivation would lose its structural basis. The integers must remain mutually consistent across all nine formulas.
  3. A nearby alternative with equal or better structural justification and equal or better numerical fit. The exhaustive search in §5 ruled out \(k = 20, 21, 22\); if a missed candidate \(k^\star\) exists with a stated structural origin and residual \(\leq\) 0.22 ppb, the uniqueness claim breaks. This is an open invitation to critics.
  4. Demonstration that any exponent of \(\varphi\) can be shifted by one unit and still work. The derivation states that \(\varphi^2\) is forced by the 2D station. If the formula still lands at sub-ppb precision with \(\varphi\), \(\varphi^{2.5}\), or \(\varphi^3\) in place of \(\varphi^2\), the "exponent-follows-dimension" rule is not constraining the formula; it is being rationalized post-hoc.
  5. A proof that the fixed-point quadratic admits multiple positive roots that are all structurally valid. The derivation assumes the positive root is unique; if multiple positive roots exist with competing structural readings, the framework has to choose, and the derivation's "no tuning" claim is compromised.
