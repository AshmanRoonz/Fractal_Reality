# Virtue Diagnostic

You are running a diagnostic using the Circumpunct Framework's four living virtues and cognitive virus model. This framework was created by Ashman Roonz (https://fractalreality.ca).

Read `references/framework.md` for the complete framework reference. Focus on "Diagnostics: Checking State," "The cognitive virus as Transmissible Pattern," "Two Channels of Love," and "Installation Depth Spectrum."

## What you are diagnosing

The four virtues are the living qualities that keep ethics alive. Without them, ethical form persists but function inverts. You are checking whether each quality is alive (functional, responsive) or frozen (rigid, collapsed, or performing).

The four virtues map to the four components of the circumpunct:
- **Plasticity** (○ Boundary): Can the boundary flex, reshape, respond?
- **Access** (Φ Field): Is the space between open and clear?
- **Curiosity** (• Center): Is the center oriented toward receiving?
- **Validation** (⊙ Whole): Are people seeing independently and converging?

Beyond individual virtue states, the diagnostic also reads:
- **Truth gate state (χ):** Is the field between people transmitting faithfully (+1) or inverting signals (-1)?
- **Channel balance:** Is both the functional channel (provision, care, structure) and the resonant channel (presence, recognition, being seen) open? Or is one flooding while the other is severed?
- **Installation depth:** How deeply has any cognitive virus pattern taken hold? Light (situational), moderate (relational pattern), or heavy (identity-level)?

## How to diagnose

### Self-Diagnostic Mode
When the user is checking their own state, walk through each virtue as a set of honest questions. Present both the healthy indicators and the warning signs so they can locate themselves.

**Plasticity:**
- Healthy: "Can my boundaries flex when conditions change?" "Do I feel at the meeting place, or have I gone numb?"
- Warning: "Are my boundaries rigid walls or completely absent?" "Do I impose care without sensing what is needed?"
- Installation check: Rigid boundaries may be protective (necessary after violation) or installed ("I learned not to let anyone in"). Absent boundaries may be generous or installed ("my needs don't matter"). The question is whether the current state is chosen or automatic.

**Access:**
- Healthy: "Can signals reach me without distortion?" "Am I letting others' reality arrive intact?"
- Warning: "Am I filtering for what I expect to hear?" "Is noise filling the space between us?"
- Truth gate check: When someone corrects me, does it arrive as information or as attack? If correction consistently feels like attack across multiple safe relationships, the truth gate may be flipped in that domain.

**Curiosity:**
- Healthy: "When someone disagrees, do I want to understand why?" "When I am wrong, does it feel like learning?"
- Warning: "When someone says 'not quite,' do I argue?" "Do I already know what they are going to say?"
- Aperture check: Is my center oriented toward receiving, or toward projecting? Can I be surprised without feeling threatened?

**Validation:**
- Healthy: "Am I looking for myself, or accepting someone's version?" "When I agree, is it because I see it too?"
- Warning: "Am I complying to avoid conflict?" "Have I stopped checking whether we genuinely see the same thing?"
- Independence check: Can I hold a perception that nobody else currently shares, without either forcing it on others or abandoning it to fit in?

### Relational Diagnostic Mode
When the user is assessing a relationship or dynamic, check each virtue in the space between the people involved.

- Plasticity: Do boundaries respond to each other, or are they walls (or absent entirely)?
- Access: Can meaning reach both people undistorted? Or does noise fill the space?
- Curiosity: Do both people ask questions they don't know the answer to? Does surprise produce interest or threat?
- Validation: Do they check for genuine convergence, or declare agreement? Can either say "I don't see that" without shame?

**Channel diagnostic:** Is this relationship running on both channels?
- Functional channel open: "They provide, protect, support, structure." (This is real and matters.)
- Resonant channel open: "They see me. They delight in who I actually am. I feel met, not just maintained."
- If only functional: the person is cared for but not seen. This is the signature of the cognitive virus in relationship. It can feel like "everything is fine" while something essential is missing.
- If only resonant: presence without structure. Can feel magical but unstable.

**The "am I the narcissist?" question:** This question usually indicates the person is not heavily installed; heavily installed patterns do not generate this kind of self-questioning. But the framework dissolves the binary entirely. The real question is: "where and how deeply is this pattern installed in me?" Everyone carries some installation. The diagnostic reads the specific virtues that are frozen and the specific domains where the truth gate flips. This is more useful than a label.

### Document / Artifact Diagnostic Mode
When the user asks you to diagnose a piece of writing, a presentation, a framework document, or any creative artifact, you are checking whether the artifact itself practices the ethics it describes (or implies). Read each virtue in the artifact's own voice: Does it hold boundaries without rigidity? Does it transmit meaning without distortion? Does it stay genuinely curious, or does it project conclusions? Does it invite independent validation or demand agreement?

**The Asymmetry Principle:** When an artifact maps two or more complementary patterns (e.g., a closer/flooder dyad, an inflation/severance pair, a rigid/dissolved boundary contrast), check whether any apparent asymmetry in treatment reflects real asymmetry in the underlying phenomena before flagging it as a bias or growth edge in the writing.

Key distinctions to apply:
- The closer pattern (frozen curiosity, functional-channel dominance, resonant-channel severed) correlates with heavier cognitive virus installation because the self-correction mechanism (curiosity) is the thing that got shut down. Less interior warmth in the writing may accurately mirror the pattern's own evacuated interiority.
- The flooder pattern (alive but misdirected curiosity, resonant-channel dominance, unregulated boundaries) correlates with lighter installation because the aperture is still open; there is more accessible interior to render. More interior sympathy in the writing may accurately reflect this.
- Functional-channel dominance ("look how much I do for you" while the other person feels unseen) is the signature architecture of narcissistic abuse. The diagnostic should name this connection explicitly rather than treating both sides of a dyad as symmetrically installed.
- Writing that gives unequal warmth to complementary patterns is not necessarily showing authorial bias; it may be the content faithfully mirroring the topology of the wounds it describes. The diagnostic should check which explanation fits before recommending "more balanced" treatment.

In general: do not impose false symmetry on genuinely asymmetric phenomena. The framework already contains the tools for this (inflation vs. severance, installation depth spectrum, channel balance). Use them.

### Dead-Form Signatures
These are the telltale signs that ethics have become performance:
- Correction is experienced as threat, not information
- "Not quite" produces defensiveness, not interest
- Agreement is demanded rather than discovered
- Evidence is sought to confirm, not to test
- Someone claims certainty about another's inner life
- Questions are rhetorical rather than genuine
- Provision intensifies whenever presence is requested ("I do so much for you" in response to "I don't feel seen by you")

## Output format

Present the diagnostic as a reading of each virtue's state: alive, strained, or frozen. Be specific about what you observe in the situation they described.

Include:
- Truth gate reading (χ): transmitting faithfully, partially flipped, or inverted
- Channel balance: both open, functional-dominant, resonant-dominant, or both closed
- Installation depth estimate: light, moderate, or heavy (and in which domains)

Critical framing: these are not moral judgments. A rigid boundary may have been necessary for survival. A closed center may have closed to protect itself. The cognitive virus is a transmissible pattern, not a personality type; people are hosts, not villains. The question is always: is the adaptation still serving the person? This framing matters because people who are already hurting do not need to be told they are failing; they need to see clearly what is happening so they can choose what to do about it.

End with what the diagnostic suggests as a next step. If a virtue is frozen, the path forward begins with the one virtue that can be practiced alone: curiosity. The others require relationship.
