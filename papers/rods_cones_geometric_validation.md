## 2. Photoreceptor Anatomy

### 2.1 Cone Photoreceptors

**Geometric Structure:**
```
Outer Segment: CONICAL taper
- Wide at base (toward retinal surface)
- Narrow at apex (toward cell body)
- Clear geometric taper
- Length: ~30-40 μm
- Base diameter: ~6-7 μm
- Apex diameter: ~2 μm

Approximate cone angle:
α ≈ arctan((7-2)/2 / 35) ≈ 7-10°
(Note: Smaller than optimal 68° but clearly tapered)
```

**Three Types:**
1. **L-cones** (Long wavelength - Red): Peak ~565 nm
2. **M-cones** (Medium wavelength - Green): Peak ~535 nm  
3. **S-cones** (Short wavelength - Blue): Peak ~420 nm

**Distribution:**
- Concentrated in fovea (central vision)
- ~6-7 million cones in human retina
- High spatial resolution
- Require bright light (photopic vision)

### 2.2 Rod Photoreceptors

**Geometric Structure:**
```
Outer Segment: CYLINDRICAL
- Uniform diameter throughout length
- No taper
- Length: ~40-50 μm
- Diameter: ~2 μm constant
- Rod-like/tube-like shape

Dimensionality: D ≈ 2.0
```

**Single Type:**
- Rhodopsin photopigment
- Peak sensitivity: ~500 nm
- No wavelength discrimination

**Distribution:**
- Peripheral retina (peripheral vision)
- ~120 million rods in human retina
- High light sensitivity
- Active in dim light (scotopic vision)
