## Key observations

1. **Only v3a has zero phase sum.** The uniform-coupling construction is the one that satisfies conservation of traversal spectrally. But it kills the aperture.

2. **The i-stroke construction (v3c) produces ○ ≈ 70%.** This is either a coincidence or a hint that the boundary station's weight at the fixed point of T IS the dark energy fraction. In the framework: dark energy IS Φ_Λ (the field of the greater whole), and Φ_Λ appears as ○_λ from inside (the containing field looks like your boundary). So ○ at 70% means "the boundary contains the field of the scale above."

3. **◐ = 0.5 does NOT emerge from any of these constructions.** The balance parameter is not a property of the fixed point of T in the 4-vector representation. Either: (a) ◐ emerges at a different level (density matrix?), (b) ◐ is a property of the PROCESS not the fixed point, (c) the beat construction isn't right yet.

4. **Without normalization, |⊙|² oscillates near 1 but doesn't converge to it.** At n=200, |⊙|² = 0.9997; but it was 0.952 at n=50 and 0.962 at n=137. The norm wobbles. This means T is not quite contractive (κ has eigenvalues both above and below 1: 1.007 and 0.993). The 1 is a saddle, not a sink, in the unnormalized system.

## The representation question

The 4-vector representation may be too thin. A state ⊙ = (z_•, z_—, z_Φ, z_○) ∈ ℂ⁴ has 4 complex = 8 real degrees of freedom. After normalization, 7. The framework has 8 stations (4 structural + 4 processual); the half-integer stations (⊛, ⎇, ✹, ⟳) are compressed into the beat operators rather than living in the state vector.

A richer representation: ⊙ ∈ ℂ⁸ with one component per station (0, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5, 3, 3.5). The beats then couple adjacent stations rather than skipping the processual ones. This doubles the space and lets ◐ potentially emerge as a relationship between structural and processual components.
