## The Core Structure

The circumpunct ⊙ = Φ(•, ○) is a whole composed of three irreducible parts:

| Symbol | Name | Dimension | Role | Maps To |
|--------|------|-----------|------|---------|
| • | Aperture/Soul | 0D+1D | Singularity (0D gate) + worldline i(t) (1D string) | TRUE |
| Φ | Field/Mind | 2D | Mediating surfaces between scales | RIGHT |
| ○ | Boundary/Body | 3D | Outer container, measurable form | GOOD |
| ⊙ | Circumpunct/Whole | All | Compositional wholeness | AGREEMENT |

Conservation law: (0+1)(•) + 2(Φ) = 3(○). Soul + field = boundary.

### Critical Terminology Distinction

- **Surface** = 2D = Φ = field = mind (the totality of 2D surfaces within a system)
- **Boundary** = 3D = ○ = body (the outer container)
- These are NOT interchangeable. §5A (The Surface Theorem) establishes: Surface = Field = Mind.

### Two Senses of "Aperture"

- **• as aperture (local)**: the gate at the center of any ⊙ through which convergence passes to emergence
- **⊙ as aperture (global)**: the whole circumpunct viewed from the perspective of ∞; from outside, the entire thing is one aperture in the foam

Both are correct; they describe the same structure at different scales.
