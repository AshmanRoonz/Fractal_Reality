The most thoroughly documented case. DFA α₁ ≈ 1.0 corresponds, for the heart rate signal, to fractal dimension D ≈ 1.5 of the RR profile (1/f noise regime). Cohort statistics: healthy young adults cluster at D = 1.48 ± 0.05.

Healthy HRV is not stability; it is fractal variability at exactly D = 1.5. Peng and Goldberger's clinical observation that "both too little and too much variability are lethal" is the two-lies diagnostic in cardiological vocabulary. This is the only scale where D ≈ 1.5 is not yet independently confirmed by this framework. Three complications distinguish it from the three closed scales:

1. **The visible/dark-matter/dark-energy split is already ⊙ Derived**, but at ◐ ≠ 0.5; the 12/64/171 ratio is a *structural* split of the i-cycle quadrants, not a balance assertion. The universe-scale D is a *different* observable and must be measured on a different signal.
2. **Galaxy two-point correlation** gives D ≈ 2 at intermediate scales (1 to 100 Mpc) transitioning to D ≈ 3 (homogeneity) at very large scales (Pietronero, Sylos Labini, Montuori and others). This is the distribution of convergence points (the 0s in the 1), not the fractal of the 1 itself. It does not falsify D = 1.5; it measures a different quantity.

CMB great-circle traces have been reported with fractal dimensions near 1.5 in small-scale analyses (notably Capozziello, De Laurentis and collaborators, though with significant measurement noise), but the statistical significance is not yet at the level of DNA, HRV, or neural spike trains.
