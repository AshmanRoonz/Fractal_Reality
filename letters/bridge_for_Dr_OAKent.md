### Complex IV Runs the Same Cycle (And Here It Really Is a Pump)

You already know this, but let me say it in framework terms:

### The Action Potential: The Clearest Biological Snap

Before we get to the heart, consider the neuron. The action potential is the clearest biological example of loading → threshold → snap → release:

```
LOADING:     Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase builds ion gradient
             Stimulus depolarizes membrane toward -55 mV
             
THRESHOLD:   -55 mV reached
             Point of no return
             
*SNAP*:      All-or-nothing depolarization to +30 mV
             Irreversible once triggered
             
RELEASE:     Signal propagates down axon
             Refractory period prevents backward flow
```

The refractory period is the **ratchet**—it ensures signals only go forward. This is why action potentials propagate in one direction. The pump has a tooth.
