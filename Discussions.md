## What I'm Looking For

### 1. **Mathematical Rigor**
- Are the derivations internally consistent?
- Are there hidden assumptions I haven't acknowledged?
- Do the fractional derivatives and aperture transformations hold up under scrutiny?
- Where does the formalism break down?

### 2. **Experimental Predictions**
- What testable predictions does this framework make that differ from Standard Model + GR?
- What experiments could falsify this approach?
- Are there predictions that are testable with current technology?

### 3. **Connections to Existing Frameworks**
- How does this relate to String Theory, Loop Quantum Gravity, Asymptotic Safety?
- Are there formal mappings to established approaches?
- What can this framework learn from successful elements of other theories?

### 4. **Identification of Errors**
- Where am I wrong?
- What experimental results contradict predictions?
- What mathematical steps are unjustified?

## What I'm NOT Looking For

- General skepticism without specific technical objections
- "This violates [physics principle]" without showing where/how
- Metaphysical debates about whether geometry is "fundamental"
- Arguments from authority ("Physicists would have found this")

## How to Engage

### If you're a physicist/mathematician:
**Open a Discussion in one of these categories:**

- **Q&A**: Technical questions about specific derivations

- **General**: Conceptual questions or broader implications

**Open an Issue if:**
- You've found a mathematical error
- You've identified an experimental contradiction
- You have a specific falsifiable prediction to propose
- You want to suggest a focused improvement

### If you're learning/exploring:
- Read the [Quick Start Guide](README.md) first
- Check existing discussions before asking questions
- Be specific: "I don't understand section 3.2" is better than "This is confusing"
- Link to specific equations or passages when asking questions

## Ground Rules

**1. Argue with the work, not the person**
- "This derivation assumes X without justification" ✓
- "You clearly don't understand quantum mechanics" ✗

**2. Be specific**
- "The top quark mass prediction is off by 15%" ✓
- "Particle physics doesn't work this way" ✗

**3. Cite sources**
- "According to PDG 2024, the measured value is..." ✓
- "Everyone knows this is wrong" ✗

**4. Acknowledge uncertainty**
I'm not claiming this is definitely right. I'm claiming it's worth serious examination because it makes testable predictions from minimal assumptions. If you engage critically but fairly, I'll do the same.
