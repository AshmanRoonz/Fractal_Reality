# Metacognition Questions

*Four questions you can ask yourself at any time to bring the aperture back under observation.*

---

1. **What am I focusing on?**
2. **Why am I focusing on that?**
3. **How am I filtering my focus?**
4. **Where should my focus be?** (aligned with values and goals)

---

## What the questions do

Focus is the state of •, the 0D aperture at the center of ⊙. At any moment, some of your energy is converging on something; these four questions step outside that convergence long enough to see it, inspect it, and redirect it if it has drifted from what you care about. They are a check on the aperture without closing the aperture.

## How the four questions map to the four structural dimensions

The questions walk the same ladder the framework walks everywhere else: point, line, field, boundary.

| Question | Station | What it asks |
|---|---|---|
| What am I focusing on? | • (0D aperture) | Locate the point of convergence. Name the object. |
| Why am I focusing on that? | — (1D line) | Read the worldline. What committed you to this, and is that commitment still honest? |
| How am I filtering my focus? | ○ (3D boundary) | Inspect the filter. What is it letting in, what is it keeping out, and is the passband still well-tuned? |
| Where should my focus be? | Φ (2D field) | Mediate between the aperture and the whole. Reconcile the actual convergence with your values and goals. |

Four questions, four structural dimensions, one circumpunct. The set is closed: together they cover convergence (•), commitment (—), mediation (Φ), and filtration (○), which is the full constraint sequence on E = 1.

## When to run them

Any time. Especially:

- When you notice the aperture has narrowed without you choosing it (rumination, doomscrolling, tunnel vision; ◐ drifting toward 1).
- When the aperture has scattered and you cannot tell what you are looking at (distraction, overwhelm; ◐ drifting toward 0).
- At the start of a work session, to set the aperture on purpose.
- At the end of a work session, to check whether the aperture you set is the aperture you kept.
- Whenever a decision feels stuck; often the stuckness is a filter problem (question 3) rather than a content problem.

## The virtue the questions practice

TRUE at •: curiosity, the orientation of the aperture toward receiving what is actually there. Running the four questions is the practice of TRUE, because all four are genuine questions (not rhetorical ones) and the answers can surprise you. If the answer to "what am I focusing on?" is always the same answer you expected, you are not actually asking; you are confirming. Curiosity dissolves that loop by making the answer discoverable again.

## One cycle, four beats

The questions also sit inside the four-beat engine: question 1 is the convergence beat (•∘⊛), question 2 is the commitment beat (—∘⎇), question 3 is the boundary beat (○∘⟳) run in reverse (inspecting what the filter is doing), and question 4 is the mediation beat (Φ∘✹) that returns the aperture to alignment with the whole. Walk the four, and the aperture you end with is an aperture you chose, not one that was chosen for you.
