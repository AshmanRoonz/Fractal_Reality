HOW EYES WERE BUILT
--------------------
Billions of years ago, there were no eyes. There were cells.
Some of those cells happened to be sensitive to light. Not
because they wanted to see. Not because anyone designed them
to see. Light hit them, and they responded. That was all.

But the ones that responded slightly better survived slightly
more often. Over millions of generations, that tiny advantage
compounded. The light-sensitive patch became a pit. The pit
became a cup. The cup developed a lens. The lens focused.

Light built the eye. The signal built the encoder.

No one designed the retina. No one chose cone cells or rod
cells. The structure emerged from the signal, through
iteration, over time.


HOW EARS WERE BUILT
--------------------
Same story. Pressure waves in air. Cells that happened to
vibrate when pressure hit them. Over time, those cells became
specialized. Membranes became eardrums. Bones became ossicles.
Fluid-filled tubes became cochleas.

Sound built the ear. The signal built the encoder.

This is not metaphor. This is literally what is happening
