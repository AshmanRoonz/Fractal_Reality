# Server Rules; please read before posting

Welcome. This server runs on the Circumpunct Ethics framework (see the pinned ethics post for the long version).

## The agreement

Three lines. Everything else on this page is practical application.

> **Truth first. Lies never.** Adjust the resolution of your truth-telling according to what's appropriate.
>
> **Everyone has access to Truth/Reality.** Treat each other as such.
>
> **Steelman:** help each other articulate the truth they are experiencing.

That's the whole agreement. The numbered rules below exist because moderators need specifics to enforce, but every one of them descends from those three lines. If you find a rule that contradicts those lines, the lines win and the rule is wrong (please flag it).

**By participating in this server you accept the agreement above.**

---

## 1. Respect every member

Disagreement is welcome; contempt isn't. No slurs, no hate speech, no targeting of anyone based on race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexuality, disability, religion, neurotype, body, or appearance. This isn't about politeness theater; it's that contempt closes the truth gate and nothing else works after that.

## 2. No harassment

Don't follow people across channels to argue with them. Don't dog-pile. Don't use alt accounts to get around blocks. If someone asks you to stop, stop. Repeat offenders get removed.

## 3. No threats, no doxxing, no incitement

No threats of violence, real or "joking." Don't share anyone's personal info (address, workplace, phone, real name unless they've shared it themselves) without explicit permission. Don't post calls to brigade, raid, or attack other communities or individuals.

## 4. Keep it SFW

This is a public community. No sexual content, no gore, no shock images. If something is borderline, just don't.

## 5. Truth gate stays open

You can disagree with anything anyone says here, including me. But: state the position you're disagreeing with **the way the original poster would phrase it**, not a version they'd reject. (Steelman first, push back second.) Strawmanning, repeated bad-faith reframing, and "you said X" when they didn't say X will be moderated as harassment.

## 6. No spam, no ads, no scams

No unsolicited DMs to recruit, advertise, or pitch. No invite links to other servers without mod permission. No NFT/crypto promotion. No referral spam. No "get rich" schemes. Friends sharing related projects in the right channel is fine; cold-marketing to strangers isn't.

## 7. Stay on-topic in topic channels

Each channel has a purpose. Off-topic chat goes in `#general` (or wherever your server's general is). Posting Pyro builds in `#wall-pattern-lab` is annoying; people are trying to find pattern stuff. Use threads when a side-conversation is taking over.

## 8. No cheating, exploits, or trainers in multiplayer

If you find a bug or exploit, please report it to a mod or in `#bugs` so it can be fixed. Don't share trainers, modified clients, aimbots, or wallhacks. Multiplayer rests on hit-consensus and good faith; cheating breaks both. Bans are permanent for confirmed cheating.

## 9. Don't fork the game (yet)

Last Ship Sailing is in active development. Please don't fork the HTML, mirror it, rebrand it, or republish it. Once the game hits a stable 1.0 it'll open up properly. Until then please share the link to the official build only. Clipping gameplay, screenshots, posts, feedback, all encouraged. (The Circumpunct Ethics framework that lives in a separate repo is CC-BY-4.0 and IS free to fork with credit; that's a different scope.)

## 10. Don't impersonate

Don't pretend to be a mod, the dev, another community member, or any real person. Don't use a profile picture or display name designed to confuse people about who you are. If you're using AI assistance to write your posts, that's allowed but please don't pass it off as someone else's voice.

## 11. AI / bot disclosure

If you're posting AI-generated content (images, text, code, music), it's fine; just say so. Don't claim AI output as hand-built work, don't deepfake other community members, don't run unauthorized bots in the server.

## 12. Use mod tools when you need help

If something or someone is making the server a worse place, ping `@Moderators` or open a ticket in `#mod-help`. Don't try to moderate other members yourself; that's how spirals start. Mods will handle it.

## 13. Receipts matter (reliability)

If you accuse someone of something, be ready to back it up with screenshots, message links, or a clear timeline. The truth gate cuts both ways: false accusations get treated as harassment, same as the thing being falsely accused.

## 14. Doomed-state grace

People have bad days. If a usually-fine community member is being short or sharp once, give them room before assuming the worst. (This is the "shy plant" rule; don't park your ship on top of them.) Repeat patterns are different from one bad night.

## 15. Discord ToS / age

You must be 13+ (or the minimum age in your country, whichever is higher) per Discord's Terms of Service. We follow Discord's TOS in addition to these rules; nothing here permits anything Discord forbids.

## 16. Mods have final call

These rules can't anticipate every situation. Mods will make judgment calls in good faith; if you disagree with a call, DM a mod calmly and we'll talk it through. Public arguments about moderation usually escalate; private appeals usually resolve.

## 17. Bans are not the end

If you've been removed and want back in later, you can DM a mod once enough time has passed and explain what's changed. Curiosity (about your own behavior) is the way back. Defensiveness is the tell that not enough has changed yet.

---

## How to agree

React to this post with ✅ to confirm you've read and accept the rules. That unlocks the rest of the server.

If anything in here is unclear, ask in `#mod-help` BEFORE you react. Once you've reacted you've agreed; "I didn't read it" stops working as a defense after that.
