# Circumpunct Ethics; the framework this server runs on

Welcome. This post is the long-form version of how we think about right action here. Read it once, lurk on it, come back to it when something feels off. The whole framework lives at https://fractalreality.ca and the plugin/code is at https://github.com/AshmanRoonz/Fractal_Reality (CC-BY-4.0 if you want to remix).

Ethics here isn't a list of rules imposed from outside. **Ethics is the pattern of what can last.** A behavior that erodes trust, distorts truth, or breaks the people involved is unethical not because someone said so, but because it can't sustain itself. A behavior that keeps trust, transmits signal cleanly, and lets people grow is ethical because it WORKS over time.

The framework has five pillars. They are checked in sequence; you can't skip ahead. Each pillar has a living virtue that keeps it alive (a pillar without its virtue collapses into performance).

---

## The Five Pillars

### 1. GOOD (Boundary) + Plasticity

Before anything else, **check boundaries.**

- Does this preserve what matters?
- Are everyone's boundaries respected (not just the loud person's)?
- Is consent honored, meaning the foreseeable effects of crossing each boundary are within what each person understands and accepts?
- Are important distinctions preserved, or collapsed into false equivalence?

The living virtue is **plasticity.** Boundaries that can't flex when the situation changes have become control, not care. Walls that never move stop being protection and start being a cage. If you find yourself defending a rule against the actual person in front of you, the rule has become rigid.

### 2. RIGHT (Field) + Access

Only after boundaries are clear can you check the path.

- Is the action proportionate?
- Does cause honestly connect to effect?
- Are real consequences being accepted, or externalized onto someone else?
- Is this grounded in how things actually work, not how someone wishes they worked?

The living virtue is **access.** Right action needs signals to travel undistorted. Cherry-picked evidence, gaslighting, "you're misunderstanding me" used to dodge, all close access. If the truth can't reach the people in the room, no amount of "the right thing" actually lands.

### 3. FAITHFUL (Line) + Reliability

Only after the path is clear can you check whether commitment has held over time.

- Has commitment been sustained, or does it appear and disappear?
- Is there a track record of follow-through (the receipts)?
- When things got hard, did the line hold?

The living virtue is **reliability.** Faithfulness is not a feeling; it is the line that doesn't break. Curiosity is a present-tense orientation; reliability is a historical record. Someone can be curious right now and still be faithless over time. Check the receipts.

### 4. TRUE (Center) + Curiosity

Only after the line has been checked can you ask what's actually seen.

- Does this correspond to reality?
- Is it honest, accurate, coherent?
- Are the people involved being authentic, or performing a role?
- Would this survive the test of clear seeing if all pretense were removed?

The living virtue is **curiosity.** Are the people involved oriented toward receiving what's actually there, or projecting, closed, frozen in their position? The test: **does correction produce interest (genuine) or defensiveness (performed)?**

### 5. AGREEMENT (Whole) + Validation

Only after truth is visible can genuine agreement emerge.

- Do the people involved genuinely resonate?
- Is there real consent, mutual recognition?
- Have they engaged with each other's reality, or just their IDEA of each other?
- Is this agreement discovered through independent seeing, or demanded?

The living virtue is **validation.** Are two (or more) people seeing independently and finding the same thing, or is one person's version being imposed while the other complies? **Compliance is not agreement.**

## The Truth Gate (chi)

Every transmission has a parameter we call chi: **does the signal pass through faithfully (chi positive), or does it invert (chi negative)?**

Faithful transmission: "you said X, I heard X, I respond to X."

Inverted transmission: "you said X, I report you said Y, I argue against Y." (Strawmanning. Bad-faith reframing. Goalpost moving.)

When chi inverts, every other pillar collapses. Truth can't pass; access is closed; agreement becomes coercion. **If you notice the gate inverting in a conversation, name it; don't push through.**

## Two Channels of Love

There are two distinct channels and they serve different purposes:

- **Functional love (provision):** I keep you fed, safe, warm, paid. The "doing" channel.
- **Resonant love (presence):** I see you, I'm with you, I respond to who you actually are. The "being-with" channel.

Both are needed. The cognitive virus weaponizes one against the other: "I provide for you, therefore you owe me presence" or "I'm so present with you, why should I have to follow through on anything?" **Watch for the swap.** Functional without resonant becomes transactional; resonant without functional becomes hollow.

## The Cognitive Virus

A transmissible pattern that installs through power-imbalanced relationships. It doesn't live in a person; it lives in the **pattern between people.** The host can't tell they're carrying it because it rewrites their perceptual calibration: anything that threatens the virus reads as an attack on the host.

Markers:
- Performed virtue (saying the right things in public, acting differently in private)
- Truth-gate inversion (you said X, they argue against Y)
- One-sided "rules" that bind everyone except the carrier
- Chronic "I'm the real victim here" framing during accountability
- Repurposing the framework's own language to dismiss the framework

If you notice these patterns (in yourself, in someone else, in a public figure, in a brand), the diagnostic isn't "you're bad" or "they're bad." The diagnostic is **the pattern needs to be seen.** Restoration is possible; performance can become living virtue again, but only after it's been seen for what it currently is.

## How we use this here

This is a Discord server, not a courtroom. Nobody's running an audit on you. But:

- **When something feels off, name the layer it's off at.** Not "you're being toxic" (vague), but "I think the truth gate is inverting" or "I'm not seeing reliability over time" or "this rule is rigid where it needs plasticity." Specific reads make conflict tractable.
- **When you disagree, steelman first.** Build the strongest version of the other position before you push back. If you can't articulate it well enough that they'd say "yeah that's what I meant," you're arguing with a strawman.
- **When YOU get pushed back on, check whether you're feeling curiosity or defensiveness.** Defensiveness is the tell. Curiosity is the way through.
- **Cost is not the same as exploitation.** Parenting, sacrifice, hard work, all cost energy. The test is whether everyone involved can still function and grow after. If yes, it's love. If no, it's drain.
- **Adaptations are not failures.** Frozen virtues, rigid boundaries, closed centers; these often started as survival strategies. The question is never "why are you broken" but "is this still serving you."
